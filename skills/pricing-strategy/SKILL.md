## Fundacao 1 — Pricing Models Foundations (8 Modelos Classicos)

Pricing model = formula como empresa converte valor entregue em revenue. 8 modelos classicos pre-SaaS continuam relevantes 2026 mas com twists modernos:

**1. Cost-Plus Pricing** (custo + markup):
- Formula: COGS + markup% (typical 20-100% B2B, 100-300% B2C luxo)
- Quando usar: commodities, utilities, contratos governo, baixa diferenciacao
- Quando NAO: SaaS, software, services com IP forte, marcas premium
- Critica Hermann Simon: "cost-plus e abandonar valor — voce cobra pelo input, nao pelo outcome"
- Brasil 2026: ainda dominante PMEs servicos (consultoria, contabilidade)

**2. Competitive/Market-Based Pricing** (benchmark concorrencia):
- Formula: precos concorrentes +/- 5-15% dependendo posicionamento
- Quando usar: mercados maduros comparable products, M&A pricing, novo entrante categoria estabelecida
- Quando NAO: produto inovador sem comparable, posicionamento premium claro
- Risco: race-to-the-bottom, Bertrand competition spiral
- Exemplo B2B SaaS: HubSpot vs Salesforce Marketing Cloud, Slack vs Microsoft Teams

**3. Value-Based Pricing** (willingness-to-pay):
- Formula: WTP customer - alternativa proxima (next-best-alternative)
- Quando usar: produto diferenciado, ROI mensuravel, B2B enterprise, SaaS premium
- Best practice 2026: 33% B2B SaaS claim value-based mas < 10% executa rigoroso
- Captures 15-35% mais revenue vs cost-plus (Maxio benchmark 2026)
- Cross-skill: pesquisa-mercado-fundamentos + persona-icp-deep (entender WTP por segment)

**4. Dynamic Pricing** (real-time adjusted):
- Formula: algoritmo factors (demand, supply, time, segment, competitor)
- Quando usar: airlines, hotels, ride-share, e-commerce SKUs alta rotacao, surge events
- Quando NAO: B2B contratos longos, SaaS subscription (gera churn), categoria regulada
- Brasil 2026: iFood surge precing horario pico, Uber, 99, Booking
- Risco LGPD: discriminacao algoritimica + Art. 20 LGPD revisao decisao automatizada

**5. Penetration Pricing** (low entry, escalar):
- Formula: preco abaixo mercado para market share rapido
- Quando usar: launch mercado novo, network effects (Slack early days), hardware com lock-in software
- Quando NAO: marca premium, subsidio impossivel longo prazo
- Cuidado: anchor baixo dificulta upsell — Slack usou + acabou trocando para usage-based

**6. Skimming Pricing** (high entry, descer):
- Formula: preco premium launch, reduz ao longo time/competition
- Quando usar: produto inovador early adopters dispostos pagar (Apple iPhone launch), marcas luxo
- Quando NAO: mass market sensitive price
- Apple skimming: iPhone $999 → $799 6 meses → $499 12 meses

**7. Bundling Pricing** (combos):
- Formula: bundle price < soma componentes (typical -15-30%)
- Quando usar: produtos complementares, perceived savings, simplificar decisao
- Tipos: pure bundle (so bundle), mixed bundle (bundle ou avulso), tying bundle (X requer Y)
- SaaS exemplo: Adobe Creative Cloud (PSD+AI+ID+...), Microsoft 365 (Word+Excel+Teams+OneDrive)
- Risco antitrust: tying excessivo (CADE Brasil, FTC EUA, EU antitrust)

**8. Freemium Pricing** (free tier permanente + paid):
- Formula: limite uso/feature/seat free + paid tiers escalar
- Quando usar: network effects, low marginal cost (SaaS), viral loop, long sales cycle B2B
- Quando NAO: high COGS, sem viral, competitive imitation rapida
- Conversion benchmark 2-5%, best-in-class 8-15% (Slack 30%+ historic, Notion 5-8%, Calendly 8%, Spotify 46% paid mas inclui anuncios)

Cross-skill: dominio-financeiro-mkt (margem unit economics), competitive-intelligence (pricing concorrencia tracking), pesquisa-mercado-fundamentos (TAM/segmentation pricing).

## Fundacao 2 — Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter (PSM)

Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter (PSM) = tecnica pricing research desenvolvida 1976 economista holandes Peter van Westendorp. Mais usada B2C/SaaS early-stage por simplicidade vs Conjoint Analysis.

**4 questoes obrigatorias** (ordem fixa, exatas):

1. **Too Cheap (muito barato)**: "Em qual preco esse produto seria tao barato que voce duvidaria da qualidade?"
2. **Cheap/Bargain (barato/pechincha)**: "Em qual preco esse produto seria uma pechincha — bom valor pelo dinheiro?"
3. **Expensive (caro)**: "Em qual preco esse produto comecaria a parecer caro mas voce ainda consideraria comprar?"
4. **Too Expensive (muito caro)**: "Em qual preco esse produto seria tao caro que voce nao consideraria comprar?"

**4 pontos resultantes** (intersecoes graficos cumulativos):

- **Point of Marginal Cheapness (PMC)** — intersecao "too cheap" + "expensive" → limite inferior aceitavel (abaixo = qualidade duvidosa)
- **Point of Marginal Expensiveness (PME)** — intersecao "cheap" + "too expensive" → limite superior aceitavel (acima = nao compra)
- **Indifference Price Point (IPP)** — intersecao "cheap" + "expensive" → preco mediano percebido como "fair"
- **Optimal Price Point (OPP)** — intersecao "too cheap" + "too expensive" → preco com menor resistencia psicologica

**Range de precos aceitavel** = entre PMC e PME (zone of acceptable pricing).

**Sample size minimo**:
- 200 respondentes minimo (significancia estatistica)
- 500+ ideal (segmentation por persona/uso)
- B2B nicho: 100-150 aceitavel se ICP bem definido

**Quando usar Van Westendorp**:
- Produto novo sem comparable (descobrir range aceitavel)
- Produto categoria existente para validar/ajustar pricing
- Pre-launch checkpoint sanity-check
- Budget research limitado USD 2k-10k (vs Conjoint USD 15k-50k+)

**Quando NAO usar**:
- Decisao tier mix (use Conjoint)
- Trade-off feature vs feature (use Conjoint)
- Mercado B2B enterprise complexo multi-stakeholder (use Conjoint + value stories)
- Produto > 5 attributes pricing-relevantes (Conjoint melhor)

**Criticas Van Westendorp** (per Conjointly, OpinionX 2026):
- Hypothetical bias (respondentes nao gastam dinheiro real)
- Over-stating WTP top 20% / under-stating bottom 20%
- Sem dimensionamento demand (so range aceitavel)
- Sem comparison contra alternativas (vs Conjoint que forca trade-off)

**Extensions** (Newton/Miller/Smith — NMS):
- Adiciona 2 questoes 5-point Likert: "Likelihood compra at cheap price?" + "Likelihood compra at expensive price?"
- Permite estimar demand curve aproximada
- Martin Rayner Interpolation (MRI) e Roll/Achterberg (RA) — outras extensoes

**Tools 2026**:
- SurveyMonkey (built-in Van Westendorp module)
- Conjointly (specialized pricing platform USD 99-499/mes)
- Qualtrics (enterprise USD 1k-15k+/mes)
- SurveyKing (free tier)
- Quantilope (enterprise consumer insights)

Cross-skill: pesquisa-mercado-fundamentos (research methodology), persona-icp-deep (segment WTP).

## Fundacao 3 — Conjoint Analysis (Trade-Off Methodology)

Conjoint Analysis = metodo statistico desenvolvido 1971 Paul Green/Vithala Rao (Cornell/Wharton) que mede preferencias customers forcando trade-offs entre attributes (incluindo preco). Recomendado #1 metodo pricing research B2B SaaS (per Contribution.dk, Conjointly, B2B International).

**Logica trade-off**:
- Em vez de perguntar "quanto pagaria por X?" (hypothetical bias), apresenta scenarios competitivos forcando escolha
- Exemplo SaaS: "Voce escolheria Plan A (5 seats, 10GB, basic support, USD 49/mes) ou Plan B (10 seats, unlimited, premium support, USD 99/mes)?"
- Repetir 12-20 scenarios com attributes/levels variando
- Statistical extraction: part-worth utilities por attribute level + relative importance attributes

**Tipos Conjoint**:
- **CBC (Choice-Based Conjoint)** — recomendado SaaS B2B 2026, mais realistico (escolhe 1 entre 3-4 options)
- **ACBC (Adaptive CBC)** — adapta scenarios baseado respostas previas, mais accurate enterprise
- **MaxDiff** — ranking attributes (best-worst) sem preco, ideal pre-Conjoint feature priorization
- **Traditional/Ratings-based** — legado, menos usado 2026

**Estrutura projeto Conjoint** (per Sawtooth, Conjointly, Drive Research):

1. **Define attributes** (4-7 max, > 7 fadiga respondente):
   - Pricing
   - Feature set (basic/standard/premium)
   - Support level (community/email/24x7)
   - Seat/usage limits
   - Contract length
   - Integration depth

2. **Define levels per attribute** (3-5 levels):
   - Pricing: USD 29 / USD 49 / USD 99 / USD 199
   - Support: community / email business hours / 24x7 phone

3. **Sample size**:
   - 200-300 minimo SaaS/B2C
   - 300-500 ideal segmentation
   - 500-1000 enterprise multi-segment + decoy testing
   - Custo respondentes B2B IT decision-makers: USD 50-200/respondente (USD 15k-100k+ projeto)

4. **Field execution** (3-6 semanas):
   - Online survey 12-20 scenarios
   - 8-12 minutos completar
   - Quality screen attention checks

5. **Analysis** (2-4 semanas):
   - Hierarchical Bayes estimation part-worth utilities
   - Market share simulator (build "what-if" scenarios price/feature changes)
   - Segment-level utilities (cluster analysis personas)

**Tools 2026 Conjoint**:
- **Sawtooth Software** (gold standard enterprise) — USD 5k-50k licenca + USD 15k-100k projeto via consultoria
- **Conjointly** (online accessible) — USD 99-2.500/projeto self-serve
- **Qualtrics CoreXM** (enterprise) — USD 5k-50k/ano licenca
- **SurveyMonkey + Conjoint plugin** (limited) — USD 50-500/mes

**Quando usar Conjoint**:
- Decisao tier mix Good-Better-Best (qual feature em qual tier)
- Pricing model selection (per-seat vs usage-based vs hybrid)
- Pre-launch feature prioritization
- Repricing 18-24 meses pos-launch validation
- M&A acquisition target pricing rationalization

**Quando NAO usar Conjoint**:
- Budget < USD 15k (use Van Westendorp DIY)
- Sample size < 200 viable (use customer interviews qualitativos)
- Produto < 4 attributes pricing-relevantes (overkill)
- Decisao critica curto prazo (Conjoint demora 8-12 semanas)
- Mercado nicho < 100 ICP buyers (statistical noise)

**Critique Conjoint** (per Ibbaka — value stories alternative):
- Stated preference vs revealed preference gap
- Multi-stakeholder B2B enterprise (Conjoint single-respondent)
- Sem context use case real
- Alternative: value stories interviews qualitativos profundos 15-30 customers

Cross-skill: pesquisa-mercado-fundamentos (research methodology rigor), persona-icp-deep (segment-level utilities), competitive-intelligence (alternatives mapping).

## Fundacao 4 — Value-Based Pricing (Hermann Simon + Ramanujam Monetizing Innovation)

Value-based pricing = pricing baseado willingness-to-pay customer percepcao valor, NAO custo nem competition. Best practice 2026 SaaS B2B mas executado rigoroso < 10% empresas (vs 33% claim).

**Hermann Simon** (Simon-Kucher founder, "Pope of Pricing"):
- Funded 1985 Simon-Kucher & Partners (now Simon-Kucher), maior pricing consultancy mundo, 2.000+ consultants 30+ paises
- Books: "Confessions of the Pricing Man" (2015), "Hidden Champions" (2009), "Power Pricing" (1996, with Robert Dolan)
- Quote signature: "Pricing is the manifestation of strategy."
- Core thesis: pricing reveals strategic positioning — premium price = premium value, discount price = volume strategy

**Madhavan Ramanujam + Georg Tacke** (Simon-Kucher partners):
- Book: "Monetizing Innovation: How Smart Companies Design the Product Around the Price" (Wiley, 2016)
- 10.000+ Simon-Kucher projects basis
- Core thesis: design produto AO REDOR do preco, NAO precificar pos-build

**9 Pricing Mistakes Ramanujam/Tacke** (Monetizing Innovation 2016):

1. **Feature Shock** — produto com features demais sem core value claro → cliente confuso, nao paga premium
2. **Minivation** — feature great mas incremento nao justifica price premium → undercharging
3. **Hidden Gem** — feature valuable enterrada no produto → cliente nao percebe
4. **Undead** — produto/feature que ninguem quer mas team nega → desperdicio R&D
5. **Pricing strategy crime: cost-plus** — pricing baseado custo, ignorando WTP
6. **Pricing crime: undercharging premium tier** — top tier mal precificado
7. **Pricing crime: complex packaging** — > 5 tiers ou > 20 features comparison
8. **Pricing crime: discount addiction** — cultura desconto cronico erodindo margem
9. **Pricing crime: no segmentation** — same price all segments (enterprise + SMB + startup)

**Process Ramanujam recomendado**:
1. **WTP research PRE-build** — Conjoint ou Van Westendorp ANTES desenvolver
2. **Segmentation by WTP** — cluster customers por sensibilidade preco + valor recebido
3. **Configure-Price-Pack** — design tiers ao redor segments WTP
4. **Pricing logic explicit** — comunicar pricing logic comercial ("voce paga X porque entrega Y")
5. **CEO-level pricing committee** — pricing decisions = strategic, nao tactical sales

**Economic Value Estimation (EVE) framework**:
- Reference value = next-best-alternative price
- Differentiation value = quantified benefits over alternative (savings, productivity, revenue lift)
- Total Economic Value = Reference + Differentiation
- Capture rate = % differentiation value vendor captures (typical 15-30% premium SaaS)

**Exemplo EVE B2B SaaS CRM**:
- Reference (HubSpot Pro): USD 800/mes
- Differentiation value (custom workflow saving 15h/mes @ USD 75/h FTE): USD 1.125/mes
- Total Economic Value: USD 1.925/mes
- Premium Pricing capture 25%: USD 800 + 0.25 * 1125 = USD 1.081/mes

**Best practices 2026**:
- WTP research min 200 buyers ICP
- Segment WTP by company size, industry, use case
- Differentiation value quantified em USD/BRL nao "melhor", "mais facil"
- Sales enablement value calculator (proof points + ROI calculator)
- Annual repricing review sync with WTP shifts (inflation, competitive)

Cross-skill: dominio-financeiro-mkt (TVE quantification), persona-icp-deep (segment WTP), competitive-intelligence (next-best-alternative mapping), gtm-strategy (sales enablement value calculator).

## Fundacao 5 — SaaS Pricing 2026 (GBB + Freemium + Usage-Based + Per-Seat)

SaaS pricing 2026 evoluiu mais nos 12 meses anteriores que decade pre-2025. Drivers: AI agents reshaping value delivery, per-seat sob pressao, hybrid 43% → 61% projetado end-2026, outcome-based 47% pilots (Gartner).

**Tiered Pricing Good-Better-Best (GBB)**:
- 71% SaaS empresas usam tiered model 3 tiers (default)
- 80% optimal Good-Better-Best ratio adoption (Cobloom 2026)
- Companies com 3 tiers geram 44% mais revenue vs 1-2 tiers
- Estrutura tipica:
  - **Good** (Starter/Basic): solo/SMB, USD 9-49/user/mes ou USD 29-99/mes flat, ~20-30% adoption
  - **Better** (Pro/Business): mid-market, USD 49-149/user/mes ou USD 99-499/mes, ~50-60% adoption (sweet spot)
  - **Best** (Enterprise/Premium): enterprise, USD 149-499+/user/mes ou custom contract USD 10k+/mes, ~10-20% adoption

**Tier mix design principles** (per Cobloom, Maxio, OpenView):
- 3-5 features per tier max comparison
- Differentiator clear top tier (advanced feature, support, security, integrations)
- Upgrade triggers explicit (limit reach forcing upgrade)
- Anchor tier = "Better" (most companies should choose this)
- Decoy tier strategy: "Good" tier purposely limitado para empurrar "Better"

**Freemium**:
- 20-30% B2B SaaS empresas usam freemium 2026
- Conversion benchmark: 2-5% (median), 8-15% best-in-class (Slack 30%+ historic peak, Notion 5-8%, Calendly 8%, Zoom 6-10%, Spotify 46% inclui ad-supported)
- Quando usar: network effects, low marginal cost, viral product (collab tools, dev tools), long enterprise sales cycle
- Quando NAO: high COGS (compute-heavy AI), sem viral, B2B nicho < 10k TAM, mercado maduro
- Free tier limits: seats (3-5), storage (1-5GB), feature gates (no integrations, no priority support), usage caps (100 API calls/mes)
- Risk: free riders cost compute, AI freemium cost compute caro 2026 (OpenAI ChatGPT Free vs Plus USD 20)

**Usage-Based Pricing (UBP)**:
- 47% empresas exploring/piloting usage-based 2026 (Gartner)
- 40% enterprise SaaS will include outcome elements end-2026 (Gartner forecast)
- Modelo: charge consumption (API calls, GB processed, transactions, conversations, documents)
- Examples: Twilio (USD/SMS), AWS (USD/hour compute), OpenAI (USD/1k tokens), Snowflake (USD/credit), Stripe (% transaction)
- Pros: aligned value, low entry barrier, organic expansion (NRR 130-150%+ best-in-class)
- Cons: revenue unpredictability, customer budget anxiety, churn risk peak usage

**Per-Seat Pricing**:
- Ainda dominante 67% SaaS usa componente per-seat 2026 (ProfitWell)
- Mas pure per-seat declining → hybrid (per-seat base + usage)
- Pros: predictable revenue, simple sales motion
- Cons: nao captura value scaling com usage, "sharing seats" subverte (uma conta usada 5 pessoas)
- Examples: Salesforce (USD 25-300+/user/mes), Slack (USD 7.25-12.50/user/mes), Microsoft 365 (USD 6-22/user/mes)

**Hybrid Pricing**:
- 43% SaaS hybrid 2026 → 61% projetado end-2026 (Chargebee State of Subscriptions)
- Modelo: base fee per-seat ou flat + usage component
- Examples: Zoom (USD/host base + minutes overage), HubSpot (USD/seat base + contacts overage), Datadog (USD/host + USD/log)
- Pros: predictability + scaling + ICP filter
- Cons: complexity comunicacao, billing systems

**Outcome-Based Pricing (emerging 2026)**:
- 40% enterprise SaaS will include outcome elements end-2026 (Gartner)
- Modelo: charge per outcome delivered (revenue lift, cost saving, leads generated)
- Examples: Drift (per qualified lead), Gong (% revenue impact), Intercom Fin AI (per resolution)
- Pros: ultimate value alignment
- Cons: attribution dispute, measurement complex, vendor risk

**SaaS Pricing Examples 2026**:
- **Slack**: per-seat USD 7.25-12.50/user/mes, freemium → Pro → Business → Enterprise Grid (custom)
- **Zoom**: hybrid host fee USD 14.99/host + meeting minutes overage
- **Notion**: freemium → Plus USD 10/user/mes → Business USD 18 → Enterprise custom
- **HubSpot**: tiered USD 18-3.600/mes Marketing + contacts/seats expansion
- **Stripe**: usage-based 2.9% + USD 0.30/transaction
- **OpenAI API**: usage-based USD/1M tokens, models tiered (GPT-3.5 → GPT-4 → GPT-4o)

Cross-skill: gtm-strategy (pricing sales motion), funil-jornada (pricing por stage), metricas-marketing (NRR/GRR/CAC payback).

## Fundacao 6 — Brasil 2026 (BRL Tropicalizacao + LGPD + CDC)

Brasil 2026 SaaS B2B B2C tem especificidades pricing que vendor global ignora ao seu risco.

**BRL Pricing Display Obrigatorio (CDC)**:
- Codigo Defesa Consumidor (Lei 8.078/90) Art. 30: "Toda informacao ou publicidade, suficientemente precisa, veiculada por qualquer forma ou meio de comunicacao com relacao a produtos e servicos oferecidos ou apresentados, obriga o fornecedor"
- Pricing display USD/EUR sem BRL conversion + impostos = violacao Art. 30 + Art. 31 (oferta)
- Boas praticas: site BR display BRL primario, USD secundario opcional
- Procon-SP autuou empresas SaaS por pricing somente USD em audiencias brasileiras 2024-2025

**Tropicalizacao Pricing 2026**:
- BRL pricing tipico SaaS B2B Brasil: USD price * 4-6 (cambio + impostos + ajuste poder compra)
- Exemplo: Slack US Pro USD 8.75/user/mes → BR R$ 33-40/user/mes
- Mas alguns SaaS usam pricing 1:1 USD-BRL para premium positioning (Notion, Figma original)
- Risco "USD pricing puro": exclui 80% mercado SMB Brasil, signal "produto gringo nao prioriza Brasil"

**Impostos Brasil pricing display**:
- B2C: ICMS-ST + PIS/COFINS + ISS embutidos preco final (display "R$ 99/mes" inclui)
- B2B: NF-e com tributos discriminados, mas display marketing "a partir de R$ X" pode ser preco bruto
- Servicos digitais foreign-providers: ISS 2-5% municipal + PIS/COFINS-Importacao 9.25% + IOF + CIDE possible
- Reforma Tributaria 2026 (Lei Complementar 214/2025): IBS + CBS substituem PIS/COFINS/ICMS/ISS gradual 2026-2033 — pricing review obrigatorio

**LGPD pricing-display dynamic**:
- Art. 6 LGPD: principios necessidade + transparencia + nao-discriminacao
- Art. 20 LGPD: revisao decisoes automatizadas que afetem interesses
- Dynamic pricing baseado dados pessoais (CEP rico/pobre, browsing history, device) = risco LGPD
- Best practice: dynamic pricing baseado context anonimo (horario, demand) OK; baseado profile pessoal = consent + revisao garantida

**Cases Brasil Pricing 2026**:

- **Nubank**: zero-fee strategy desafiou bancos legacy. Sem anuidade cartao, sem TED tarifa, sem manutencao conta. Penetration pricing pura → 90M+ clientes. Now expanding premium Ultravioleta (anuidade R$ 50/mes pos-150k spend)
- **iFood**: take rate restaurante 12-23% + delivery surge pricing horario pico. Dynamic pricing chuva/futebol/frio elevou take rate efetivo. Restaurantes reclamam mas low-altrenatives
- **Magalu**: pricing dinamico marketplace + Magalu Cloud B2B SaaS 2026 entrante. Magalu PaymentLeague consolidou e-commerce
- **Stone**: SMB merchant USD 4-8/mes maquininha + 2.5-3.5% transaction. Penetration pricing vs Cielo/Rede legacy
- **MercadoLivre**: marketplace 11-19% take rate + Mercado Pago payment processing. Tiered ML Classico vs Premium
- **Hotmart**: 9.9% + R$1 take rate digital products. Volume play vs Eduzz (4.99-9.9%) e Kiwify

## Anti-Patterns 18

1. **Cost-plus default sem WTP research** — abandona 15-35% revenue na mesa
2. **Pricing decided pos-build** — Ramanujam crime #1, design produto NAO ao redor preco
3. **Race-to-bottom matching concorrencia** — erode margem, sinaliza commodity, sem upside
4. **Discount addiction cronico** — > 20% deals discounted = pricing list errado, nao "sales agressivo"
5. **Tier sprawl > 5 tiers** — paralisa decisao, complica sales motion
6. **Free tier sem viral hook** — burn compute sem expansion path
7. **Per-seat puro produto value-scales-com-usage** — sharing seats subverte, NRR limitado
8. **Pricing sem segmentation** — same price enterprise vs SMB = cannibalize ou nao captura
9. **Annual increase 0% historico** — inflation eroga margem, eventual catch-up shock
10. **Van Westendorp N=30 hypothetical bias amplificado** — sample inadequada
11. **Conjoint sem segment analysis** — average masks variance critica
12. **Pricing sem CFO/CEO involvement** — decisao tactical sales = local optimum
13. **USD-only pricing Brasil** — exclui SMB, sinaliza desprezo mercado, viola CDC Art. 30
14. **Dynamic pricing pessoal LGPD-violator** — Art. 20 revisao + Art. 6 nao-discriminacao
15. **Pricing page hidden ("contact sales") tier basico SMB** — lead waste, qualification falha
16. **Communication pricing logica ausente** — "porque sim" mata trust + objection handling
17. **Sunset legacy tier brutal** — grandfather pricing 12-18 meses minimo
18. **Pricing review > 24 meses gap** — mercado evolui, competitor moves, value drift

## 18 Regras de Ouro

1. **Design produto AO REDOR do preco** (Ramanujam) — WTP research PRE-build
2. **Pricing = manifestacao estrategia** (Hermann Simon) — nao afterthought tactical
3. **Sample N >= 200 pricing research** — abaixo = ruido statistico
4. **3 tiers default Good-Better-Best** — 80% adoption ratio benchmark
5. **Anchor "Better" tier** — most companies should choose middle
6. **Decoy strategy "Good" tier limitado** — empurra para "Better"
7. **Differentiator clear top tier** — feature/support/security/SLA
8. **Annual review pricing obrigatorio** — quarterly discount audit, semi-annual mix
9. **Discount > 20% deals = pricing errado** — fix list price, nao banalize discount
10. **Value-based > cost-plus > competitive** — captura 15-35% mais revenue
11. **Conjoint > Van Westendorp > customer interviews qualitativos** — accuracy ladder
12. **BRL display obrigatorio Brasil** — CDC Art. 30 + tropicalizacao tributaria
13. **Hybrid pricing direction 2026** — base + usage, 43% → 61% projetado end-2026
14. **Outcome-based emerging** — 47% pilots, ultimate value alignment
15. **CEO-level pricing committee** — strategic, nao tactical sales
16. **Pricing comunicacao explicita** — value calculator + ROI proof + transparent logic
17. **Cross-skill mandatory** — pesquisa-mercado-fundamentos + persona-icp-deep + dominio-financeiro-mkt + competitive-intelligence
18. **Disclaimer educacional** — pricing decision require advogado fiscal + CFO + CEO context

## Checklist Contraditorio Interno (10 perguntas)

Antes entregar pricing recomendacao, perguntar honestamente:

1. **WTP research base?** Conjoint N>=200 ou Van Westendorp N>=200 ou customer interviews N>=15 qualitativo? Ou guessing/cost-plus/competitive matching?
2. **Segmentation considerada?** WTP por size empresa, industry, use case? Ou average mascarando variance?
3. **Value-based logic explicit?** Reference value + differentiation value + capture rate quantificados? Ou "porque sim"?
4. **Tier mix design rigor?** 3-5 tiers max, anchor "Better", decoy "Good", differentiator clear top? Ou tier sprawl?
5. **Cannibalization risco modelado?** Tier upgrade map + grandfather plan + comms strategy? Ou launch cego?
6. **Brasil tropicalizacao?** BRL display + tributos contemplados + Reforma Tributaria 2026 IBS/CBS impact + CDC Art. 30 compliance? Ou USD-puro nao-CDC?
7. **LGPD dynamic pricing OK?** Pricing context anonimo OK; baseado profile pessoal = consent + Art. 20 revisao? Ou risco regulatorio?
8. **Pricing committee CEO-level?** CEO/CFO/CRO/CMO sign-off? Ou tactical sales decision local optimum?
9. **Cross-skill alignment?** pesquisa-mercado-fundamentos + persona-icp-deep + dominio-financeiro-mkt + competitive-intelligence convocados? Ou pricing isolado?
10. **Disclaimer mandatory?** Educacional + nao constitui assessoria fiscal/juridica/financeira specifica? Adviser advogado + CFO recomendacao explicit?
