## 1. Fundacao 1 — Porter Five Forces 2026

### 1.1. Framework original (Michael Porter 1979 HBR)

**5 forces shaping industry competition**:

1. **Competitive Rivalry** (existing competitors).
2. **Threat of New Entrants**.
3. **Threat of Substitute Products**.
4. **Bargaining Power of Suppliers**.
5. **Bargaining Power of Buyers**.

### 1.2. Application B2B SaaS 2026

**Industry Rivalry**:
- Fierce competition em high-stakes deals.
- NOT just price — functionalities + customer service + biggest accounts.
- Differentiation critical.

**Threat New Entrants**:
- High development costs + specialized expertise + trust building = high barriers.
- BUT open-source + cloud infrastructure lower barriers some niches.
- AI tools democratize entry.

**Threat Substitutes**:
- Game-changing tech > direct competitors.
- AI agents could obsolete entire categories.
- "There's always something new around corner".

**Bargaining Power Buyers (B2B)**:
- Few large buyers concentrated purchasing.
- Easy switching disciplines providers.
- Price-sensitive in large purchases.

**Bargaining Power Suppliers**:
- Cloud providers (AWS+Azure+GCP) = significant power.
- Specialized API providers (Stripe+Twilio) = some power.
- Talent supply tight (engineers+AI).

### 1.3. Modern relevance 2026

**Stats**:
- **Core principles remain relevant**.
- Application+interpretation **must adapt** modern markets.
- **93% companies digital-first strategy** (Foundry 2023).
- Digital transformation **intensified competitive rivalry**.

- "Threat substitutes > direct competitors — AI agents obsoleting categories".
- "B2B bargaining power buyers — large concentrated + price-sensitive".

## 2. Fundacao 2 — SWOT 2026 + AI Integration

### 2.1. SWOT origins + relevance 2026

**Albert Humphrey 1960s** Stanford Research Institute.

**Stats 2026**:
- **89% Fortune 500 strategy teams still use SWOT**.
- **72% Fortune 500 use 2026 strategic planning cycles**.
- Businesses using SWOT **2.8x more likely achieve strategic goals**.

### 2.2. SWOT components

**Internal**:
- **Strengths** (capabilities + resources + competitive advantages).
- **Weaknesses** (gaps + limitations + vulnerabilities).

**External**:
- **Opportunities** (market trends + tech shifts + regulatory).
- **Threats** (competitors + substitutes + economic + risks).

### 2.3. AI transformation SWOT 2026

**Major shift**:
- Where teams spent **weeks gathering data** for brainstorming.
- AI tools process **10-K filings + earnings transcripts + market data** in **seconds**.
- AI **assumption testing** — every claimed strength evaluated against customer sentiment + benchmarks + metrics.
- AI **stress-test assumptions** simulating market conditions.

### 2.4. Common SWOT mistakes 2026

**Critical issue**:
- **Vague superficial statements** describing any company.
- Generic strengths NOT actionable.
- Surface-level NOT decision-useful.

**Fix**:
- Specific evidence per claim.
- Quantitative validation.
- Stress-tested assumptions.

### 2.5. TOWS Matrix extension

**TOWS** (reverse SWOT):
- **SO strategies** (Strengths × Opportunities).
- **ST strategies** (Strengths × Threats defensive).
- **WO strategies** (Weaknesses × Opportunities improvement).
- **WT strategies** (Weaknesses × Threats avoid).

**SWOT content angles**:
- "**89% Fortune 500 SWOT 2026** — still core strategic tool".
- "**AI SWOT execution** weeks → seconds (10-K+earnings+market data)".
- "**AI assumption testing** — strengths evaluated quantitatively".
- "**TOWS Matrix** — reverse SWOT actionable strategies".

## 3. Fundacao 3 — Perceptual Maps + Brand Positioning

### 3.1. Perceptual mapping definition

**Visual technique**:
- Customer perceptions visualized **2D graph** (X+Y axes).
- 4 quadrants intersection.
- Brands plotted by attributes.
- Similar perceived = close together; different = far apart.

### 3.2. Application brand differentiation

**Strategic uses**:
- **Identify market position** vs competitors.
- **Reveal unclaimed attributes** (white-space).
- **Track consumer sentiment** evolving.
- **Craft positioning statement** impactful.
- **Find "nirvana point"** = customer ideal.

### 3.3. How to build perceptual map

**Steps**:
1. Define **target market**.
2. Choose **2 attributes** important customers (e.g., price/quality, simple/complex).
3. Survey customers (rate brands per attribute).
4. Plot brands on 2D graph.
5. Analyze quadrants:
   - Crowded zones (avoid OR differentiate radically).
   - Empty zones (potential opportunity).
   - Distance to "nirvana point" (customer ideal).

### 3.4. Modern approach 2026

**Living tools**:
- **Re-cut by segment** OR occasion.
- **Refreshed new data** continuously.
- **Paired quantitative models** linking position to outcomes.
- AI-augmented analysis.

### 3.5. Aplicacao em content marketing

**Perceptual maps content angles**:
- "Perceptual maps 2D quadrants — visual brand differentiation".
- "**'Nirvana point'** customer ideal — distance reveals gap".
- "**Unclaimed attributes** = white-space opportunities".
- "**Living tools 2026** — re-cut+refresh+pair models".

## 4. Fundacao 4 — Competitive Benchmarking 2026

### 4.1. 5 types benchmarking

**Type 1 — Competitive**: vs direct rivals.
**Type 2 — Strategic**: business models + long-term positioning.
**Type 3 — Process**: operational workflows.
**Type 4 — Performance**: output KPIs (revenue + market share).
**Type 5 — Internal**: across own business units.

### 4.2. SaaS Triangle + Critical KPIs 2026

**Executive Scorecard typical**:
- **Gross Margin 75%+** (cross-skill `dominio-financeiro-mkt`).
- **CAC Payback <15 months** (B2B SaaS healthy).
- **NRR 101%+** (Net Revenue Retention).

**Other key metrics**:
- AVG B2B SaaS CAC: **$1,200**.
- B2B SaaS sales cycle 2026: **134 days** (+25% vs previous years).
- Conversion benchmarks: 1.5-2.5% visitor-to-lead, 45-55% lead-to-MQL top performers.

### 4.3. Two-level reporting 2026

**Successful SaaS approach**:

**Executive scorecards** (high-level):
- Long-term health.
- Investor-grade metrics.
- Quarterly review.

**Operational dashboards** (detailed):
- Day-to-day execution.
- Leading indicators.
- Weekly review.

### 4.4. Tracking frequency

- **Digital signals** (pricing+ads+jobs): **weekly OR continuous**.
- **Financial+strategic**: **quarterly**.
- **Full competitive review**: **twice/year minimum**.

### 4.5. Critical principle 2026

**Comparison set RELEVANT**:
- Same industry.
- Similar deal size.
- Comparable GTM motion.

**Wrong**: SaaS enterprise vs SMB-focused = invalid.

### 4.6. Aplicacao em content marketing

**Benchmarking content angles**:

- "**SaaS Triangle 2026** — GM 75%+ / CAC payback <15m / NRR 101%+".
- "**B2B SaaS sales cycle 134 days** — +25% vs previous years".
