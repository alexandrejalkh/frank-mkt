## 3. Big Idea — definicao + foundational

### 3.1 Definicao Wikipedia

```
BIG IDEA (marketing/advertising)

"Term used to describe the core concept behind:
   - an advertising campaign
   - a brand
   - a product

Creating a strong message that resonates

= UNIFYING NUCLEUS
= EMOTIONAL ANCHOR

```

### 3.2 Ogilvy vs Bernbach — 2 philosophies

```
DAVID OGILVY (Ogilvy & Mather, 1948+)

PHILOSOPHY
   "Content is more important than how it's said"
   Research-driven (Reader's Digest method)
   Long-copy ads ("The man in the Hathaway shirt")
   Brand-as-personality
   Discipline + facts + product knowledge

ICONIC CAMPAIGNS
   - Hathaway shirts (eye patch)
   - Rolls-Royce ("60 mph clock")
   - Dove (original "1/4 moisturizing cream")
   - Schweppes (Commander Whitehead)

ICONIC QUOTE
   "If it doesn't sell, it isn't creative."

BILL BERNBACH (Doyle Dane Bernbach DDB, 1949+)

PHILOSOPHY
   "Advertising is an art, not a science"
   Creativity > research
   Emotional connection > rational claims
   Breaking conventions
   Visual + verbal integrated

ICONIC CAMPAIGNS
   - Volkswagen "Think Small" (1959 — small ad)
   - Volkswagen "Lemon" (admitted defects)
   - Avis "We Try Harder" (#2 positioning)
   - Levy's Real Jewish Rye

ICONIC QUOTE
   "Properly practiced creativity can make ONE ad
    do the work of TEN."
```

### 3.3 Convergence — both agree

```
DESPITE PHILOSOPHIES, BOTH OGILVY + BERNBACH AGREED

CORE PRINCIPLE
   "What is the IDEA, what is the CONCEPT,
    what is the NUGGET?"

= focus on SINGLE central concept
= memorable + distinctive
= emotionally resonant
= specific to brand

This timeless wisdom drives Big Idea
to this day (2026)
```

## 4. Bernbach Creative Revolution — 5 revolutions

### 4.1 Bernbach 5 ways revolutionised advertising

```
1. CREATIVE TEAMS (copywriter + art director paired)

   PRE-1949: copywriters and art directors siloed
   
   BERNBACH INNOVATION: paired in teams of 2
   = visual + verbal integrated from start
   = collaboration > sequential handoff
   
   STILL STANDARD 2026
   = creative team = canonical structure

2. CREATIVITY OVER RESEARCH

   PRE: market research dictated direction
   
   BERNBACH: "creativity is the most practical
              thing a businessman can employ"
   
   IMPLICATION
   = research INFORMS but doesnt DICTATE
   = leap of faith for breakthrough ideas
   = data tells past, creativity makes future

3. EMOTIONAL CONNECTION

   PRE: rational claims dominated
        ("4 of 5 dentists recommend...")
   
   BERNBACH: emotion + humor + provocation
   
   EXAMPLES
   - Volkswagen "Think Small" (counter-intuitive)
   - Avis "We Try Harder" (humility)
   - "Lemon" (admitted defect)
   
   IMPLICATION 2026
   = emotional brands win (Liquid Death, Oatly,
     Duolingo, Wendy's = direct descendants)

4. BREAKING CONVENTIONS

   PRE: industry templates rigid
   
   BERNBACH: violate rules deliberately
   = stand out via difference
   
   EXAMPLES
   - Volkswagen ad small (industry was big bold)
   - Black-and-white (when color was norm)
   - Self-deprecating humor (when brands serious)

5. PERMISSION TO FAIL (creative culture)

   BERNBACH: "Take risks. Reward courage."
   
   = creative culture > approval culture
   = bold work > safe work
   = innovation > imitation
```

### 4.2 Bernbach iconic quotes

```
"Properly practiced creativity can make ONE ad
 do the work of TEN."

"Logic and over-analysis can immobilize and sterilize
 an idea. It's like love — the more you analyze it,
 the faster it disappears."

"It's not just what you say that stirs people. It's
 the way that you say it."

"At the heart of an effective creative philosophy is
 the belief that nothing is so powerful as an insight
 into human nature."

"The most important element in advertising is the
 product itself."
```

### 4.3 Aplicacao 2026

```
BERNBACH PRINCIPLES STILL APPLY

✅ Pair copy + designer (or content + visual)
✅ Research informs, creativity decides
✅ Emotional > rational (95% subconscious cf. `branding`)
✅ Break conventions deliberately (Liquid Death formula)
✅ Permission to fail (Edmondson "Right Kind of Wrong")
   cf. `persuasao-eticas` Sec 5.3
```

## 5. StoryBrand Donald Miller — customer-as-hero

### 5.1 Core insight

```
DONALD MILLER "Building a StoryBrand" (2017)

Based on Joseph Campbell's Hero's Journey
(monomyth foundational pattern)

CORE SHIFT
   Brand thinks: "WE are the hero"
   StoryBrand: "CUSTOMER is the hero, BRAND is the guide"

WHY THIS WORKS
   Audiences identify with HERO (themselves)
   Audiences trust GUIDES (have been there)
   Brand-as-hero = brand-centric (audience tunes out)
   Brand-as-guide = audience-centric (audience leans in)

FAMOUS QUOTE
   "If you confuse, you lose."
```

### 5.2 7-part framework

```
ELEMENT 1: A CHARACTER
   = customer (hero)
   Has a desire (wants something)
   Goal must be specific + clear

ELEMENT 2: WITH A PROBLEM
   = pain point or obstacle
   3 levels:
   - External (surface problem)
   - Internal (emotional pain)
   - Philosophical (why this matters)

ELEMENT 3: MEETS A GUIDE
   = brand
   Demonstrates EMPATHY + AUTHORITY
   "We've been there + we know how to help"

ELEMENT 4: WHO GIVES THEM A PLAN
   = simple steps
   3 steps ideal (cognitive ease)
   Plan removes confusion

ELEMENT 5: AND CALLS THEM TO ACTION
   = clear CTA
   Direct ("Buy now") OR Transitional ("Watch demo")

ELEMENT 6: THAT RESULTS IN SUCCESS
   = positive outcome
   Specific imagery of better life

ELEMENT 7: OR FAILURE (stakes)
   = consequence of not acting
   Avoidance motivation
   Loss aversion (cf. `persuasao-eticas` Sec 6.2)

= HERO'S JOURNEY adapted to marketing
```

### 5.3 BrandScript template

```
[BRAND] helps [CUSTOMER]
who wants [DESIRE]
but is struggling with [PROBLEM]
to [PLAN/STEPS]
so they can [SUCCESS]
and avoid [FAILURE STAKES]

EXAMPLE B2B SaaS
"[Product] helps B2B sales teams
who want to close more deals
but struggle with manual lead qualification
to use AI-powered behavioral scoring
so they can hit quota every quarter
and avoid losing deals to faster competitors."
```

### 5.4 Common mistakes StoryBrand violations

```
❌ BRAND = HERO ("We are #1 platform")
✅ CUSTOMER = HERO ("You can become #1 in your market")

❌ MULTIPLE PROBLEMS LISTED
✅ ONE PROBLEM (most painful)

❌ BRAND = GURU EXPERT (intimidating)
✅ BRAND = GUIDE empathetic + authoritative

❌ COMPLEX PLAN
✅ 3-STEP PLAN (cognitive ease)

❌ NO STAKES
✅ FAILURE CONSEQUENCES NAMED

❌ VAGUE CTA
✅ DIRECT + TRANSITIONAL CTAs both
```

## 6. 3 cases iconicos — deep dive

### 6.1 Apple "Think Different" (1997)

```
CONTEXT
   Apple near-bankruptcy 1997
   Steve Jobs returned as CEO
   Brand identity crisis

CONTEXT COMPETITIVE
   IBM slogan: "Think"
   Apple response: "Think Different"

THE BIG IDEA
   Celebrate "the crazy ones"
   Einstein, Gandhi, MLK, John Lennon, Picasso, etc.
   Emotional ode to icons who saw world differently

EXECUTION
   - 60-second TV spot
   - No products shown (radical for tech)
   - Black-and-white footage
   - Powerful narration ("Here's to the crazy ones...")
   - Print campaign with same icons
   - Posters

INSPIRATION
   Steve Jobs cited NIKE ads as best example
   "They never talk about the product."
   "They honor great athletes and great athletics."

RESULT
   - Apple stock rose
   - Brand re-energized
   - Customers identified with creators/changemakers
   - Set tone for next 25+ years Apple marketing
   - Cultural touchstone (still referenced 2026)

LESSONS
   1. Big Idea > product features
   2. Celebrate AUDIENCE (customer-as-hero indirect)
   3. Emotional > rational (subconscious 95%)
   4. Bold simple core ("Think Different")
   5. Sustained execution decades
```

### 6.2 Nike "Just Do It" (1988)

```
CONTEXT
   1988 Nike vs Reebok rivalry
   Reebok dominating women's fitness
   Nike needed broader appeal

THE BIG IDEA
   "Just Do It" — universal call to action
   Action over excuses
   Empowerment for everyone, not just elite athletes

DEBUT EXECUTION
   Walt Stack — 80-year-old runner
   Crossing Golden Gate Bridge daily
   "Just Do It" mindset belonged to EVERYONE
   Raw + gritty (NOT polished)

INSPIRATION
   Wieden+Kennedy creative director Dan Wieden
   Inspired by death row inmate Gary Gilmore's last words:
   "Let's do it"
   Translated to "Just Do It"

RESULT
   - Sales: $800M (1988) → $9.2B (1998) +1050%
   - Cultural rallying cry
   - Transcended sports
   - Slogan evolved into philosophy
   - Continues 2026 (38+ years)

LESSONS
   1. 3-word universal hook
   2. Show diverse "athletes" (anyone with body)
   3. Anti-polish authentic
   4. Empowerment > features
   5. Sustained brand voice 38+ years
```

### 6.3 Dove "Real Beauty" (2004)

```
CONTEXT
   Dove brand stagnant
   Beauty industry homogenized
   Women alienated from beauty advertising

INSIGHT (research-driven, Ogilvy-style)
   "Only 2% of women considered themselves beautiful"
   = massive insight + emotional disconnect

THE BIG IDEA
   "Real Beauty" — celebrate women as they are
   No retouching
   All shapes/sizes/ages/ethnicities
   Authentic > aspirational

EXECUTION
   - Print + video ads with REAL women
   - "Evolution" viral video (model transformation)
   - "Real Beauty Sketches" forensic artist
   - Self-Esteem Project (educational programs)
   - Sustained 20+ years (2004-present)

RESULT
   - Sales: $2.5B (2004) → $4B+ (2014)
   - Cultural conversation shifted
   - Other brands followed (CVS no-retouching policy)
   - Set bar for purpose-driven branding
   - Won Cannes Lions multiple
   - Revolutionized 21st century PR

LESSONS
   1. Insight-driven (research informs creativity)
   2. Cultural moment timing critical
   3. Sustained 20+ years (NOT one-off)
   4. Real (vs models) = emotional resonance
   5. Purpose authentic + ACTION (Self-Esteem Project)
```

### 6.4 Common patterns 3 cases

```
WHAT THEY SHARED

1. SIMPLE CORE (3 words ideal)

3. EMOTIONAL HOOK

4. SUSTAINED EXECUTION DECADES
   Apple 25+ years

5. CULTURAL IMPACT
   Transcended advertising

## 8. Single-Minded Proposition (SMP)

### 8.1 Definicao

```
SINGLE-MINDED PROPOSITION (SMP)

Definition: ONE non-negotiable message
            that guides entire campaign

WHY SINGLE
   Brain caps at 1-2 attributes per brand (cf. Trout & Ries)
   Multiple messages = remembered as nothing
   Focus = memorability

= "What's the ONE thing customer must walk away with?"
```

### 8.2 Compress-to-sentence test

```
ULTIMATE TEST

"Compress the campaign into ONE SENTENCE."

If line is muddy = concept is muddy
If sentence simple enough customer/creator/journalist
   repeats accurately = concept clear

SENTENCE EXAMPLES (good)
   Apple Think Different: "Honor the crazy ones who change the world."
   Nike Just Do It: "Anyone can be an athlete. Just start."
   Dove Real Beauty: "All women are beautiful — the industry just doesn't show it."
   Old Spice Repositioning: "Be the man your man could smell like."
   Liquid Death: "Murder your thirst. Death to plastic."

BAD EXAMPLES (vague)
   "Innovative platform that empowers users."
   "Premium quality solution for businesses."
   "Industry-leading technology and service."
```

### 8.3 SMP creation workshop

```
4-HOUR WORKSHOP

HOUR 1: BRIEF REVIEW
   Positioning + branding + funnel
   Audience insight depth
   Cultural moment context

HOUR 2: BRAINSTORM 50+ SMPs
   No filters
   Volume > quality
   Cross-functional team

HOUR 3: FILTER TO TOP 10
   Sentence test (clarity)
   Audience resonance check
   Differentiation vs competitors
   Brand voice alignment (cf. `branding`)

HOUR 4: SELECT TOP 3
   Validate with stakeholders
   Test with 3-5 customers (mini)
   Final SMP recommendation
```

### 8.4 SMP examples per archetype

```
HERO ARCHETYPE (cf. `branding` Sec 3.2)
   "[Brand] = courage [audience] needs to [outcome]"
   Nike: "Just Do It"

OUTLAW ARCHETYPE
   "[Brand] = rebellion against [status quo]"
   Liquid Death: "Death to corporate water"

SAGE ARCHETYPE
   "[Brand] = knowledge that [transforms outcome]"
   Google: "Find anything you need to know"

CAREGIVER ARCHETYPE
   "[Brand] = protection [audience] deserves"
   Volvo: "For life"

JESTER ARCHETYPE
   "[Brand] = joy [audience] forgets to have"
   Skittles: "Taste the rainbow"
```

## 9. Creative brief template 2026

### 9.1 Essential components

```
1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
   - Project name
   - Date + deadline
   - Stakeholders
   - Budget
   - Channels

2. BACKGROUND + CONTEXT
   - Brand status quo
   - Market context
   - Competitive landscape
   - Cultural moment

3. TARGET AUDIENCE
   - Persona detail (cf. `persona-icp` futura)
   - Demographics + psychographics
   - Audience LANGUAGE (their words)
   - Pain points + aspirations
   - Where they consume content

4. KEY CHALLENGES
   - Business problem to solve
   - Audience misperception
   - Internal obstacles
   - Risk factors

5. COMPETITORS
   - Direct + indirect (3-5 brands)
   - Their messaging
   - Gaps identified
   - Differentiation opportunity

6. STRATEGY
   - Big Idea hypothesis
   - SMP candidate

## 15. Regras de Ouro

1. **NAO CHUTAR** — Big Idea sem customer insight + sentence test = wasted production.
2. **'What is THE idea, what is the nugget'** Ogilvy + Bernbach mantra timeless.
3. **Customer = hero, brand = guide** (StoryBrand Donald Miller).
4. **'If you confuse, you lose'** — clarity > cleverness.
5. **Single-Minded Proposition (SMP)** — ONE non-negotiable message.
6. **Compress to ONE SENTENCE** test — line muddy = concept muddy.
7. **3 cases iconicos**: Apple Think Different (1997 25+ years) + Nike Just Do It (1988 38+ years) + Dove Real Beauty (2004 20+ years).
8. **Bernbach 5 revolutions**: creative teams + creativity over research + emotional + breaking conventions + permission to fail.
9. **StoryBrand 7-part**: character + problem + guide + plan + CTA + success + failure.
10. **Cannes Lions 2026** purpose-driven genuine action; AB InBev 3x Creative Marketer.
11. **Sustained execution decadas** — Big Idea = commitment, not campaign.
12. **Founder/CEO buy-in** — Big Idea = brand trajectory shift.
13. **Creative brief modern 2026** = living system + AI context-aware.
14. **Pair copywriter + designer** Bernbach lesson timeless.
15. **Cultural moment timing** critical (Pepsi Jenner 2017 lesson).
16. **BR-specific** — saudade + jeitinho + WhatsApp-first + Pix + regional sensitivity.
17. **3-5 active creative variants** testing pipeline always running.
18. **Cross-skill** com `posicionamento-marca` (anterior) + `branding` (anterior) + `funil-jornada` (anterior) + `metricas-marketing` (proxima/final).

### Exemplo 3 — "Compress to sentence test — meu concept passa?"

**Input:** "Concept: 'Inovamos com IA para empoderar empresas a transformar processos manuais em workflows inteligentes que liberam tempo para inovação estratégica.' Passa sentence test?"

**Resposta esperada:**

> NO — falha multiple sentence tests + diversos Big Idea principles. Razao: 25 palavras + 4-5 conceitos competing + buzzwords ("inovamos", "empoderar", "inteligentes", "estratégica") + brand-as-hero ("Inovamos NÓS") + vague. Apply rigorous diagnostic + revision. **Diagnostic**: (1) **Word count 25 palavras** — too long (target 10-15 max para SMP memorable); (2) **Multiple ideas competing**: (a) "innovate with AI" + (b) "empower businesses" + (c) "manual processes → intelligent workflows" + (d) "free time for strategic innovation" = 4 ideas (each diluting); (3) **Buzzword density alta**: "inovamos" (generic), "empoderar" (corporate-speak), "inteligentes" (vague), "estratégica" (vague) = sounds like 100 other B2B SaaS; (4) **Brand-as-hero violation** (StoryBrand): "Inovamos NÓS" = brand-centric vs customer-centric "Você ganha tempo strategic" (customer-as-hero); (5) **Sentence test failure**: cant be repeated accurately by customer/journalist 1 month later; (6) **Competitor test failure**: Zapier, Make, n8n, Tray.io, Workato could all say identical sentence — me-too; (7) **Customer language test failure**: customer doesnt say "transformar processos manuais em workflows inteligentes" — they say "stop wasting time copy-pasting between Slack and Salesforce" (cf. customer LANGUAGE in `multi-platform-narrative` Sec 2.2); (8) **Emotional test failure**: zero emotion + zero stakes + zero specificity = cold. **Revision approach using Bernbach + StoryBrand principles**: **STEP 1 Compress to ONE thing** (SMP — Single-Minded Proposition): what's THE ONE thing customer should walk away with?; if you say multiple things = remembered as nothing; pick ONE: (a) "save time" OR (b) "automate boring work" OR (c) "focus on strategy not busywork"; **STEP 2 Customer-as-hero shift**: instead of "Inovamos com IA" (we innovate), shift to "Você ganha [outcome]" (you gain); StoryBrand: brand = guide, customer = hero; **STEP 3 Customer LANGUAGE**: replace corporate jargon with customer words; "transformar processos manuais" = "stop copy-pasting" (specific) OR "deixe a máquina fazer trabalho de máquina" (BR translation); "workflows inteligentes" = "automações" (simpler); "tempo para inovação estratégica" = "tempo para o trabalho que importa" (emotional); **STEP 4 Cut to memorable**: target 5-10 words ideal SMP; **REVISED OPTIONS**: (a) "Reclaim your strategic time" (English original 4 words, customer-as-hero, emotional + specific); (b) "Liberte seu tempo do trabalho repetitivo" (BR Portuguese 6 words, action verb "liberte" emotional); (c) "Pare de copiar e colar. Comece a estratégia." (BR provocative 8 words split with directness Bernbach-style); (d) "Você não foi contratado para trabalho de robô" (provocative 8 words, customer-recognition strong, emotional truth); (e) "Trabalho repetitivo sai. Estratégia entra." (binary contrast 5 words, memorable rhythm); **VALIDATION matrix top 3 candidates**: choose 3 (provavel "Liberte seu tempo do trabalho repetitivo" + "Você não foi contratado para trabalho de robô" + "Reclaim your strategic time") + apply 7 tests (Sec 14.3 desta skill — sentence + competitor + customer recognition + emotional + truth + memorability + archetype alignment Sage+Magician). **Anti-padroes que evitamos**: (a) generic "innovate" verb (every brand says); (b) brand-as-hero "we innovate"; (c) buzzword stacking "intelligent strategic"; (d) multi-message diluting; (e) corporate-speak "empower"; (f) translated US tech-speak when BR audience; (g) length excess (25 words — 3x too long); (h) zero emotional resonance; (i) copy competitor messaging structure; (j) skip customer validation antes de production. **Recomendacao final**: take top 3 revised SMPs, test with 10+ customers (BR-specific se BR market), iterate based feedback, settle on 1 final SMP that passes all 7 tests. **Cross-ref**: `posicionamento-marca` Sec 6 (positioning statement template Geoffrey Moore), `branding` Sec 5 (brand voice doc), `escrita-por-publico` Sec 4 (B2B tom — formal but not corporate-jargon-heavy), `engajamento-comunidade` (community language), `multi-platform-narrative` Sec 2.2 (audience LANGUAGE > brand language).

**Comportamento-chave:** diagnostic rigorous identificando 8 falhas + 7 tests, step-by-step revision com Bernbach + StoryBrand principles, 5 revised options especificos, validation matrix tests, anti-padrões 10 especificos.
