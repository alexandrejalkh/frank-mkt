## 1. Visao Geral

Guerrilla marketing **trades MONEY for ATTENTION** quebrando formatos esperados (Jay Conrad Levinson, "Guerrilla Marketing" 1984). Em 2026, e mais relevante que nunca por 3 razoes:

1. **Banner blindness** — consumers ignoram traditional ads (banner ads, pre-roll videos, sponsored posts)
2. **AI content flood** — generic AI content overload faz authentic creative scarce + valuable
3. **Algorithm anxiety** — plataformas mudam algoritmos; stunts memoraveis transcendem feed

**ROI quantificado massivo** (cases verificaveis):

- **Duolingo "Dead Duo" stunt** (2025): mascote killed simulado viral global = **1.7B impressions em 14 dias** (cf. `composicao-visual` Sec 9 mascote $100M+ value)
- **Burger King "Whopper Detour"** (2018-2019, ainda referenciado): **37:1 ROI** quando ordering BK ate 600 pes de qualquer McDonald's = $0.01 Whopper
- **Liquid Death**: $1.4B valuation 2026 — **canned water** virou unicorn via guerrilla branding ($110M → $263M retail 2022→2023, 3 anos triple-digit growth, IPO planejado 2026)

Esta skill cobre 6 fundacoes:

1. **Definicao + 4 canonical families** (ambient, experiential, ambush, viral).
2. **Cases iconicos analyses** — Duolingo, Liquid Death, Oatly, BK, A24, Magazine Luiza.
3. **SHARE TRIGGER framework** — "if you cannot articulate why someone would photograph your activation, go back to drawing board".
4. **ROI + measurement** — KPIs + CLV calculation.
5. **Costs structure** — SMB $1k-25k vs Enterprise $25k-1M+.
6. **Risks** — legal + reputation + execution failure.

Mais 2 aplicacoes:

7. **Brazil-specific** — Magazine Luiza Lu, BK BR, Casas Bahia legacy.
8. **Workflow operacional** — concept → execution → amplification.

## 3. Definicao + 4 canonical families

### 3.1 Levinson 1984

```
"Guerrilla marketing trades MONEY for ATTENTION
 by BREAKING the format the audience expects."

KEY ELEMENTS
   - Unexpected (violates pattern)
   - Memorable (sticks > minutes after)
   - Shareable (people pull phone out)
   - Cost-efficient (creativity > budget)
   - Brand-aligned (amplifies voice, doesn't dilute)
```

### 3.2 Family 1 — Ambient

```
PHYSICAL ENVIRONMENT TURNED INTO MEDIA

EXEMPLOS
   - Athletic brand basketball court Chicago overnight (2026)
     branded hoops + surface art + QR code drop = viral overnight
   - IKEA bus stops como showroom mini
   - Frontline flea ad stairway floor (looks like dog scratching)
   - 3M shatter-proof glass with $$ inside (NYC bus stop)
   - Volkswagen "piano stairs" (subway escalator → piano keys)

CHARACTERISTICS
   - Physical-first
   - Often public space (legal vetting!)
   - Photographable from many angles
   - Live for hours/days/weeks

COST RANGE
   $5k-150k typical
   Permissions varies (public space tricky)

RISK
   Legal (unauthorized public alterations)
   Cleanup costs
   Weather/environmental factors
```

### 3.3 Family 2 — Experiential

```
LIVE EVENT / STUNT / INSTALLATION

EXEMPLOS
   - Tom Sachs Sony Walkman 1999 NYC subway (artist stunt)
   - Coca-Cola "Happiness Machine" college (bursting cans)
   - WestJet Christmas Miracle 2013 (live gift surprise)
   - Lululemon yoga in unexpected places
   - A24 + Chalamet "Marty Supreme" 18min leaked video (2026)
   - Liquid Death pop-up "stores" airport / festival
   - Oatly "Hey Barista!" magazine launch + party

CHARACTERISTICS
   - Live human element
   - Attendees become content creators
   - Limited-time-only urgency
   - Often single location amplified globally via social

COST RANGE
   $10k-1M+
   Production-heavy

RISK
   Logistics fail (rain, no-shows)
   Brand alignment off
   Social sentiment if execution bad
```

### 3.4 Family 3 — Ambush

```
PIGGYBACK COMPETITOR/EVENT ATTENTION

EXEMPLOS
   - Burger King "Whopper Detour" (ambush McDonald's geofencing)
   - Pepsi "Pepsi Challenge" 1975 (ambush Coke blind taste)
   - DC Shoes Ken Block "Gymkhana" (ambush motorsports)
   - Newcastle Brown Ale "If We Made It" Super Bowl ambush (NAO patrocinou mas fez ad sobre nao patrocinar)
   - Heineken Champions League (ambush UEFA event)
   - Brewdog "Magic Mango" (ambush Brewdog founders publicly!)

CHARACTERISTICS
   - Reactive to competitor/event
   - Witty + cheeky tone
   - Often legal grey area
   - Can backfire if seen as "punching up"

COST RANGE
   $5k-100k
   Speed-critical

RISK
   Trademark infringement
   Defamation lawsuits
   Backlash if seen as "mean"
```

### 3.5 Family 4 — Viral

```
CONTENT ENGINEERED TO SPREAD

EXEMPLOS
   - Dollar Shave Club "Our Blades Are F***ing Great" 2012 ($4500 video, $1B exit Unilever)
   - Old Spice "The Man Your Man Could Smell Like" 2010
   - Liquid Death TikTok comedy continual
   - Duolingo "Dead Duo" 2025
   - Cards Against Humanity various stunts
   - Wendy's Twitter roasting

CHARACTERISTICS
   - Distribution-first thinking
   - Engineered to be shareable
   - Brand-aligned (Liquid Death's punk = brand voice)
   - Continual feed (not one-off)

COST RANGE
   $1k-100k per piece
   $10k-500k/year for sustained brand voice

RISK
   Tone-deaf if culture shifts (Pepsi Kendall Jenner 2017)
   "Forced viral" feels synthetic
   Hard to predict what spreads
```

### 3.6 Decision matrix

| Family | Best for | Cost | Speed | Risk |
|--------|----------|------|-------|------|
| **Ambient** | Local awareness + photogenic | $5k-150k | Slow setup, slow burn | Legal public space |
| **Experiential** | Brand experience + UGC | $10k-1M+ | Slow planning | Logistics fail |
| **Ambush** | Competitor visibility | $5k-100k | Fast reactive | Lawsuits + backlash |
| **Viral** | Continuous brand-building | $1k-100k/piece | Fast iteration | Tone-deaf |

## 5. SHARE TRIGGER framework

### 5.1 Definicao

```
"Every successful guerrilla campaign has a moment
 that makes people pull out their phone."

If you cannot ARTICULATE WHY someone would
photograph your activation, GO BACK to drawing board.

= SHARE TRIGGER must be IDENTIFIED before execution
```

### 5.2 5 categories of share triggers

```
1. STATUS-SIGNAL
   "I want to show I was here / saw this"
   Ex.: exclusive product drops, limited events, 
        unique installations
   Brands: Supreme, Liquid Death drops

2. JOKE-TO-REPEAT
   "I want to make my friends laugh"
   Ex.: clever puns, absurd visuals, mockumentary
   Brands: Cards Against Humanity, A24 Chalamet

3. PROOF-TO-FORWARD
   "Look at this thing I'm sharing as evidence"
   Ex.: comparison stunts, before/after dramatic, 
        uncovered fraud
   Brands: BK Whopper Detour, Newcastle ambush

4. AESTHETIC-CONTENT
   "This looks beautiful / cool / artistic"
   Ex.: ambient art, photogenic installations
   Brands: IKEA bus stops, athletic court Chicago

5. CONTROVERSY/DEBATE
   "I want to argue about this"
   Ex.: bold political/cultural stances
   Brands: Liquid Death anti-corporate, Oatly anti-dairy
   RISK: backfire potential alta
```

### 5.3 Share trigger test (5 questions)

```
ANTES DE PRODUCAO, PERGUNTAR:

1. WHY would someone photograph this?
   (Specific reason, NOT "because it's cool")

2. WHO is the imagined sharer?
   (Specific persona, NOT "young people")

3. WHAT would they CAPTION when sharing?
   (Specific words, draft 3 hypothetical captions)

4. HOW LONG until forgotten?
   (Realistic — 1 hour? 1 day? 1 week? months?)

5. CAN THIS BE COPIED easily by competitor?
   (If YES, time-window short — execute fast)

SE 3+ respostas vague = volta drawing board
```

### 5.4 Share trigger DESIGN process

```
WORKSHOP 4-8 HOURS

STEP 1 — IDENTIFY EXPECTED FORMAT (1h)
   What's the format audience expects from your category?
   What patterns do they "pre-skip"?

STEP 2 — INVERT / VIOLATE FORMAT (2h)
   How can you violate that format?
   Brainstorm 50+ ideas (volume > quality initially)

STEP 3 — SHARE TRIGGER FILTERING (1h)
   For each idea, identify share trigger category
   Discard ideas without identifiable trigger

STEP 4 — RESOURCE FEASIBILITY (1h)
   Within budget? Within timeline? Within risk tolerance?

STEP 5 — TOP 3 CONCEPTS (1h)
   Stakeholder review
   Pre-vet with 5+ external people

STEP 6 — DECISION (1h)
   Execute 1
   Iteration plan if first fails
```

## 6. ROI + measurement

### 6.1 KPIs guerrilla 2026

```
PRIMARY KPIs

IMPRESSIONS
   Social shares × estimated audience
   Press coverage circulation
   Foot traffic se ambient

ENGAGEMENT
   Shares (most important)
   Comments + reactions
   UGC generated
   Hashtag usage

EARNED MEDIA VALUE (EMV)
   Media coverage equivalent paid value
   Tipical multiplier 5-50x cost
   Tools: Cision, Meltwater, Critical Mention

BRAND METRICS
   Aided + unaided recall
   Brand sentiment
   Affinity lift (surveys before/after)

BUSINESS METRICS
   Sales lift attributable
   Web traffic spike
   App downloads
   Sign-ups
```

### 6.2 ROI calculation

```
TRADITIONAL ROI
   ROI = (Revenue gained - Cost) / Cost

GUERRILLA-SPECIFIC ROI
   ROI = (EMV + Sales attributable) / Total cost
   
ESTENDIDA (CLV-based)
   ROI = (CLV × New customers) / Cost
   
EXEMPLO BK WHOPPER DETOUR
   Cost: ~$1M (estimate)
   App downloads: 1.5M
   New customers: 100k+
   Avg LTV BK customer: $X
   EMV: $30M+ press coverage
   = 37:1 ROI
```

```yaml
campaign_id: GUERRILLA-2026-Q2-001

  pre_launch:

  - 1M+ impressions
  - 5x EMV vs cost
  - +10 points brand recall lift
  - +20% web traffic 30 days post
```

## 13. Regras de Ouro

1. **NAO CHUTAR** — stunt sem share trigger articulavel = waste.
2. **Levinson 1984**: trades MONEY for ATTENTION by BREAKING format.
3. **4 canonical families**: ambient + experiential + ambush + viral.
4. **SHARE TRIGGER test** — 5 questions Sec 5.3 antes de produzir.
5. **Pre-vet 5+ external** — "would you photograph this?" honestly.
6. **Risk audit 7 categories** Sec 9.3 — legal + cultural + execution + brand + stakeholder + share trigger + backlash.
7. **Diverse review** mandatory — H&M Coolest Monkey lessons.
8. **Cultural moment timing** crucial — Pepsi Jenner 2017 lessons.
9. **Brand voice consistency** — Liquid Death formula = 7+ anos consistency.
10. **Measurement plan PRE-execution** — KPIs + baselines + EMV + CLV.
11. **SMB $1k-25k** vs **Mid $25k-150k** vs **Enterprise $150k-1M+**.
12. **Earned media multiplier** target 5-50x cost.
13. **BR-specific**: humor + persona + saudades + soccer + WhatsApp + Pix.
14. **Crisis response plan** pre-drafted (real-time critical).
15. **Stunt + brand voice mismatch** = confusion (Liquid Death = punk = brand voice perfect alignment).
16. **"Forced viral" synthetic** detected = brand collapse.
17. **Continual brand voice** > one-off stunts (Liquid Death continuous content).
18. **Honesty + acknowledgement** can be powerful (BK Reclaim the Flame).
