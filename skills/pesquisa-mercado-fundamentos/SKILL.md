## 1. Fundacao 1 — TAM SAM SOM Framework 2026

### 1.1. Definitions atualizadas 2026

**TAM (Total Addressable Market)**:
- **Total demand if no limits + zero competitors**.
- "If you had 100% market share globally".
- Maximum theoretical revenue.
- Useful upper bound NOT realistic target.

**SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**:
- **Segment you can realistically target with GTM strategy**.
- Filtered by: geography + business model + capabilities + segment.
- Larger than SOM but smaller than TAM.

**SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**:
- **Realistic 3-5 years capture given competition + resources**.
- **0.5-2% max Year 1-3** (avoid unicorn projections).
- Conservative > aggressive (VCs respect).

**PAM (Potential Addressable Market)** — sometimes used:
- Future TAM if market grows.
- Optional 4th metric.

### 1.2. Two methodologies — top-down vs bottom-up

**Top-Down approach**:
- Start broad market data (Statista + Gartner + IDC + Forrester).
- Progressively narrow with filters.
- Example: Global software → SaaS → B2B → industry → segment.
- **Fast but optimistic**.
- **VCs 2026 reject** as "Statista summaries".

**Bottom-Up approach**:
- Start from real data: price + potential customers + sales capacity.
- Build up: ICP × volume × price.
- **Slower but defensible**.
- **VCs 2026 prefer** (mandatory).

**Best practice 2026** (Waveup + OpenVC):
- **Use BOTH approaches**.
- Show they **converge similar market size**.
- "Strongest decks show both — founder understands market every angle".

### 1.3. Calculation formulas

**Basic formula**:
```
TAM = Total potential customers × Annual Contract Value (ACV)
SAM = TAM × % addressable (geography + segment + capability filters)
SOM = SAM × realistic capture % (0.5-2% Year 1-3)
```

**Example B2B SaaS BR**:
```
TAM:
- Total empresas BR: 4.5M (DataSebrae 2026).
- Empresas mid-market+ ($1M+ revenue): ~150K.
- ACV target: R$ 50.000/year.
- TAM = 150.000 × R$ 50.000 = R$ 7.5 bilhões.

SAM:
- Filter setor (SaaS focus): ~30K empresas.
- Filter regiao (SP+RJ+MG): ~20K.
- ACV: R$ 50.000.
- SAM = 20.000 × R$ 50.000 = R$ 1 bilhão.

SOM (3 anos):
- Realistic capture 1.5%: 300 customers.
- ACV: R$ 50.000.
- SOM = 300 × R$ 50.000 = R$ 15 milhões/year.
```

### 1.4. VC expectations 2026

**Series A**:
- **TAM $1 billion+ minimum**.
- Justify fund economics (10x exit).

**Series B**:
- **TAM $5 billion+ minimum**.
- Larger fund-style requirements.

- Crossover funds.

**Conservative SOM**:
- 0.5-2% Year 1-3.
- 5-10% Year 5-7 max.
- "VCs respect realistic > inflated unicorn".

## 2. Fundacao 2 — Research Methodology 2026

### 2.1. Primary vs Secondary research

**Primary research** (own data):
- **Surveys** (online + phone + in-person).
- **Interviews** (structured + semi-structured + unstructured).
- **Focus groups** (in-person + virtual).
- **Observation** (ethnography + usability + field).
- **Experiments** (A/B testing + RCTs).

**Secondary research** (existing data):
- **Industry reports** (Gartner + Forrester + IDC + Mintel).
- **Government data** (IBGE BR + Census US + EU Eurostat).
- **Academic research** (Google Scholar + JSTOR).
- **Trade publications** (industry magazines + trade journals).
- **Internal data** (CRM + analytics + customer support).

### 2.2. AI integration 2026 — 95% researchers

**Stats macro 2026**:
- **95% researchers using AI** for transcription + pattern detection + theme identification (Cognitive Future).
- AI-powered interviewers conduct rich adaptive conversations thousands participants.
- **Traditional weeks → AI hours** for transcription + coding.
- Mixed methods norm (sequential + concurrent).

### 2.3. AI tool categories 2026

**Category 1 — End-to-end platforms**:
- **Quals AI** (real human interviews + insights 24h).
- **Conveo AI** (qualitative research software).
- **Listen Labs** (qualitative AI 2026).

**Category 2 — Qualitative analysis software**:
- **NVivo** (traditional + AI features).
- **Atlas.ti** (AI-augmented).
- **Dovetail** (modern AI-native).

**Category 3 — Research ops platforms**:
- **User Interviews** (recruitment + panels).
- **Respondent.io**.
- **Prolific**.

### 2.4. Mixed methods 2026

**Sequential design**:
- Phase 1: quantitative (surveys → trends).
- Phase 2: qualitative (interviews → understand why).

**Concurrent design**:
- Quantitative + qualitative simultaneous.
- Triangulation evidence.

**AI adoption shift**:
- AI-assisted coding **first-pass analysis**.
- BUT design + verification still **human judgment**.
- AI = accelerator NOT replacement.

### 2.5. Sample size considerations

**Quantitative**:
- B2C survey: **n=400 minimum** (95% confidence ±5% margin).
- B2B survey: **n=100 minimum** typical (smaller universe).
- Statistical significance critical.

**Qualitative**:
- **5-10 interviews** until saturation initial.
- **20-30 interviews** for robust findings.
- Saturation = no new themes emerging.

## 5. Fundacao 5 — Pitch Deck Market Slide 2026

### 5.1. Reality 2026 — VC standards tighter

**Major shift** (Waveup, OpenVC, Pitch Doctor):
- **2026 fundraising market tougher than 2021**.
- VCs **running tighter ICP math**.
- **Demanding bottom-up grounding**.
- **Rejecting top-down decks "Statista summaries"**.
- **Strongest decks show BOTH** approaches converge.

### 5.2. Market slide critical questions

**3 questions investors asking**:
1. **Is the market big enough?** (TAM size).
2. **Is now the right time?** (timing + tailwinds).
3. **Do you understand to win?** (depth + insights).

**One thing matters more than MVP/team/forecast**: **YOUR MARKET**.

### 5.3. Bottom-up best practices

**Underscore VC bottom-up framework**:
```
Bottom-up Market Size = Business Model × ICP Volume

Steps:
1. Define ICP precisely (segment + size + geography + behavior).
2. Calculate ICP universe (number of companies/individuals).
3. Apply business model (pricing + ACV).
4. Calculate addressable revenue (TAM/SAM/SOM).
5. Validate with public sources.
```

### 5.4. Market slide structure 2026

**Best practice slide elements**:
- **Headline thesis** (1 sentence).
- **3-tier diagram** (TAM → SAM → SOM).
- **Numbers per tier** ($, customers, geography).
- **Methodology note** (top-down + bottom-up).
- **Source citations** (Tier 1 + Tier 2).
- **Growth rate** (CAGR).
- **Now timing** (why this moment).

### 5.5. Anti-patterns market slides 2026

**5 erros comuns 2026**:

**1. Top-down only** (Statista summary).
**2. TAM as headline** (SOM matters more for execution).
**3. No methodology** (just numbers).
**4. Inflated SOM** (>5% Year 3).
**5. Generic sources** (random blog posts).

## 6. Fundacao 6 — B2B SaaS Market 2026 example application

### 6.1. B2B SaaS macro 2026

**Stats macro** (Mordor Intelligence):
- **$492.34 billion 2026** market size.
- **$1.578 trillion 2031** projected.
- **CAGR 26.24%** 2026-2031.
- **Drivers**: gen-AI copilots + cloud-first + usage-based pricing.

### 6.2. Top-down B2B SaaS sizing example

**Top-down approach**:
- Global software market ($1.7T) → SaaS share (~30%) = ~$500B SaaS.
- B2B SaaS (~80% SaaS) = ~$400B (close to Mordor estimate).
- BR market (~2% global SaaS) = ~$10B BR.

### 6.3. Bottom-up B2B SaaS sizing example

**Bottom-up B2B SaaS BR healthtech**:
```
ICP: Healthcare providers BR mid-market+ (>50 beds OR >$2M revenue).
Universe: ~3.500 hospitais + clínicas qualified.
ACV target: R$ 60.000/year per facility.

TAM healthtech BR SaaS: 3.500 × R$ 60.000 = R$ 210M (NICHE).

SAM (filter regiao+digital maturity): 1.500 × R$ 60.000 = R$ 90M.

SOM 3 anos (1.5%): 22 × R$ 60.000 = R$ 1.3M.
SOM 5 anos (5%): 75 × R$ 60.000 = R$ 4.5M.
```

### 6.4. Convergence test

**Best practice convergence**:
- Top-down: $10B BR SaaS × 5% healthcare = $500M (TAM healthtech).
- Bottom-up: R$ 210M (TAM healthtech ACV-based).

**Discrepancy**: ~5x.
**Conclusion**: top-down inflated for niche; bottom-up more accurate.

## 7. Fundacao 7 — Common Mistakes + Calibration

### 7.1. 10 erros comuns market sizing 2026

**1. Top-down only** (Statista summary).
**2. TAM headline** (SOM matters execution).
**3. No methodology** disclosure.
**4. Inflated SOM** (>5% Year 3 unrealistic).
**5. Generic sources** (random blogs vs Tier 1).
**6. Ignore competition** (zero competitor assumption).
**7. ACV = list price** (vs realistic discounted ACV).
**8. Mature market** as if greenfield.
**9. Geographic over-claim** (global without proof).
**10. No CAGR mention** (static market unrealistic).

### 7.2. Calibration techniques

**Triangulation 3 methods**:
1. Top-down (broad → narrow).
2. Bottom-up (price × volume).
3. Comparable (similar company benchmark).

**If 3 converge**: solid number.
**If divergent**: investigate why.

### 7.3. Sensitivity analysis

**Show 3 scenarios**:
- Conservative (low ACV + low capture).
- Base (realistic).
- Aggressive (high ACV + high capture).

VCs respect: **conservative-realistic > aggressive-only**.

- "**Triangulation 3 methods** — top-down + bottom-up + comparable".
