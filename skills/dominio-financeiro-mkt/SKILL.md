## 5. Fundacao 5 — SaaS unit economics + capital efficiency 2026

### 5.1. Burn Multiple — David Sacks framework

**Formula** (David Sacks 2020):
- **Burn Multiple = Net Burn / Net New ARR**.

**Benchmarks 2026** (Runway, SaaS Capital):
- **<1x = excellent** (efficient growth).
- 1-1.5x = good.
- 1.5-2x = okay.
- 2-3x = struggling.
- >3x = inefficient warning.

**Example**: Net burn €1M / Net new ARR €400k = **Burn Multiple 2.5x** (struggling).

**Win profile 2026**: Burn Multiple **<1.5x = tight ship**.

### 5.2. Cash runway benchmarks 2026

**Cash runway por stage**:
- $10M-$50M ARR: **25 meses runway median**.
- <$10M ARR: 18 meses runway.
- >$50M ARR: 18 meses runway.

**Por que $10-50M tem mais runway**:
- Still raising VC capital.
- Burn multiple targets (efficient).
- Profitability path clearer.

### 5.3. Winners profile SaaS 2026

**Win companies 2026 profile**:
- **CAC payback <15 meses**.
- **Burn Multiple <1.5x**.
- **Gross margins >75%**.
- **ARR per employee** climbing year-over-year.

**Bootstrapped vs VC-backed 2026** (SaaS Capital):

| Metric | Bootstrapped $3-20M ARR | VC-backed $10-50M ARR |
|--------|-------------------------|------------------------|
| Revenue growth | 15% median | 30-50% median |
| NRR | 103% median | 110-120% median |
| GRR | 91% median | 90-95% median |
| Cash runway | varies (profitable) | 25 meses median |

### 5.4. NRR + GRR 2026

**Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** (cross-skill `dominio-negocios-mkt`):
- <100% leaky bucket DANGER.
- 100% maintaining.
- 110-120% outstanding.

**Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)**:

## 6. Fundacao 6 — Capital structure 2026 — equity + venture debt

### 6.1. SAFE Notes — pre-seed/seed staple

**SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)** — Y Combinator 2013:
- **Pre-seed/seed fundraising staple 2026**.
- Founders convert investment para equity future round.
- Simpler vs convertible notes (no interest + no maturity).
- **Move to priced equity Series A+**.

**SAFE terms typical 2026**:
- Valuation cap (max conversion price).
- Discount (ratio next round price).
- MFN (Most Favored Nation — match better terms).
- Pro rata (right participate next round).

**SAFE limitations**:
- No interest accrued.
- No maturity (founder ambiguity).
- Cap table complexity if multiple SAFEs.

### 6.2. Venture Debt 2026 — landscape

**Venture debt market** (Re:cap):
- US **$53.3 billion 2024** (+94.5% YoY vs $27.4B 2023 record).
- **Deal count dropped lowest decade** (lenders selective).

**Mechanics**:
- **25-35% of last equity round** non-dilutive (or minimally-dilutive).
- Interest rates **8-15%** (SOFR + 6-9%).
- **Warrants 1-5% dilution** (typical sweetener).

**Quando faz sense venture debt**:
- Post-Series A (proven traction).
- Extend runway between rounds.
- Bridge to profitability (last money pre-IPO).
- Capital-efficient growth (NOT replace equity).

**Quando NAO**:
- Pre-Series A (lenders avoid).
- High burn (default risk).
- Distressed (worsens situation).

**Players**:
- Silicon Valley Bank (recovered post-2023).
- Bridge Bank.
- Hercules Capital.
- TriplePoint Capital.
- Pinnacle Bank.
- BR-specific: Banco Modal + BTG Pactual venture lending emergentes.

### 6.3. Cap table 2026 — dilution evolution

**Cap table benchmarks 2026** (CRV):

**Seed stage**:
- Founders combined: 70-85%.
- Investors: 15-25%.
- Option pool: 10-15%.

**Series A**:
- Founder team ownership **median 36%** (down de 70% pre-A).
- VCs: 20-30%.
- Option pool refreshed: 10-15%.

**Series B**:
- Founder team: 25-30%.
