## 1. Fundacao 1 — Blue Ocean Strategy 2026

### 1.1. Origin + thesis

**Kim & Mauborgne 2004 HBR + 2005 book**:
- Blue Ocean = uncontested market space + make competition irrelevant.
- Red Ocean = bloody fierce competition existing markets.
- **2026 Enhanced Edition forthcoming** (Kim+Mauborgne).

**Authors credentials**:
- INSEAD professors of strategy.
- INSEAD Blue Ocean Strategy Institute Fontainebleau France.
- **2023 honored 4 leading thinkers** 100 years HBR publication.

### 1.2. Value Innovation core concept

**Definition**:
- Simultaneous **differentiation + low cost**.
- NOT trade-off either/or.
- "Leap in value" buyers + sellers.
- Aligns innovation + utility + price + cost.

### 1.3. 4 Actions Framework

**ERRC Grid**:
- **Eliminate**: which factors industry takes for granted?
- **Reduce**: which factors below industry standard?
- **Raise**: which factors above industry standard?
- **Create**: which factors industry never offered?

### 1.4. Strategy Canvas

**Visualization tool**:
- X-axis: factors industry competes on.
- Y-axis: offering level customers receive.
- Plot competitors + your value curve.
- Identify divergence points = blue ocean.

### 1.5. Six Paths Framework

**Paths discovery blue ocean**:
1. Look across alternative industries.
2. Look across strategic groups within industry.
3. Look across chain of buyers.
4. Look across complementary product/service offerings.
5. Look across functional+emotional appeal.
6. Look across time (trends).

### 1.6. Examples blue ocean

**Classic examples**:
- **Cirque du Soleil** (circus + theater fusion).
- **Yellow Tail wines** (simple+fun vs sophisticated).
- **Nintendo Wii** (motion control + casual gamers).
- **Tesla** (electric + premium + tech early days).

**2026 examples**:
- **OpenAI ChatGPT** (consumer AI assistant blue ocean).
- **Notion** (database + docs + wiki integrated).
- **Airbnb** (existing inventory + community + experience).

## 2. Fundacao 2 — White Space Analysis Methodology

### 2.1. Definition

**White space analysis**:
- Strategy uncover growth opportunities.
- Find gaps between offerings + actual customer needs.
- Address use cases unaddressed.
- "Where company efforts can count most".

### 2.2. 3 types gaps

**Functional gaps**:
- Existing solutions address jobs partially OR poorly.
- "Job to be done" framework integration.
- Examples: software too complex, missing features.

**Emotional gaps**:
- Customer feelings unacknowledged.
- Empathy lacking products.
- Examples: shame buying, frustration UX.

**Occasion gaps**:
- Specific contexts unserved.
- Time/place/situation unique needs.
- Examples: Sunday cooking, business travel breakfast.

### 2.3. Frameworks white-space

**SWOT + Gap Analysis** (most grounded):
- Real-time competition context.
- Market gaps identification.

**Ansoff Matrix**:
- Market Penetration.
- Product Development.
- Market Development.
- Diversification.

**Visual Mapping**:
- Customer needs + competitor offerings.
- Visual aid prominence.

### 2.4. Data triangulation Euromonitor approach

**Multi-source**:
- Passport data.
- Product audits.
- Retailer interviews.
- Social listening.
- Consumer surveys.
- Expert interviews.

### 2.5. Discovery techniques 2026

**Audience gaps**:
- Everyone targets beginners — nobody intermediate/advanced.
- White-space underserved segment.

**Format gaps**:
- Every product video course — nobody hands-on templates.
- Format innovation opportunity.

**Price gaps**:

**Geographic gaps**:
- Major markets covered — secondary cities ignored.
- Regional opportunity.

**Search volume + social signals**:

## 3. Fundacao 3 — ODI Opportunity Algorithm 2026

### 3.1. Origin + framework

**Tony Ulwick 1991** (Strategyn CEO+founder):
- ODI **30+ years applications**.
- **1.500+ consulting engagements** Fortune 1000.
- **86% success rate vs 17% industry** (5x improvement).

### 3.2. Opportunity Algorithm formula

**Formula**:
```
Opportunity = Importance + (Importance - Satisfaction)
```

**Score interpretation**:
- **>15** = extreme opportunity (must address).
- **12-15** = low-hanging fruit (improvement ripe).
- **<12** = saturated OR low priority.

### 3.3. ODI process

**5 steps**:
1. **Define market** at job level (NOT product/customer level).
2. **Discover customer needs** (50-150 desired outcomes per market).
3. **Quantify importance + satisfaction** (large-sample survey).
4. **Calculate opportunity** scores per outcome.
5. **Innovate solutions** addressing high-opportunity unmet needs.

### 3.4. Outcomes vs needs distinction

**Outcomes** (Ulwick):
- Specific, measurable, controllable, action-independent.
- Example: "Minimize the time it takes to find an item".
- Stable over time (NOT solution-specific).

**Needs** (vague):
- "I want better experience".
- Solution-biased.

### 3.5. Aplicacao em content marketing

**ODI content angles**:
- "**Opportunity Algorithm** — Importance + (Importance - Satisfaction)".
- "**>15 extreme + 12-15 low-hanging fruit** — score interpretation".
- "**ODI 86% success vs 17% industry** — 5x improvement".
- "**Outcomes vs needs** — specific measurable controllable".
- "**5 steps ODI** — define+discover+quantify+calculate+innovate".

## 4. Fundacao 4 — Disruptive Innovation Christensen

### 4.1. Origin + thesis

**Clayton Christensen early 1990s HBS**:
- Coined "disruptive innovation" term.
- **NOT breakthrough technologies** making good products better.
- **Make products + services more accessible + affordable**.

### 4.2. Two types disruption

**Low-end disruption**:
- Bottom of market entry.
- "Good enough" customers.
- Lower profit margins.
- Incumbents move "upstream".

**Examples low-end**:
- Online booksellers (Amazon initially).
- 3D printing real estate ($4-10k home).
- BYD EVs (China cheap quality).

**New-market disruption**:
- Compete against **non-consumption**.
- Lower margin sectors.
- New customers entirely.

**Examples new-market**:
- Netflix DVDs-by-mail (initially non-threat Blockbuster).
- Streaming model (eventually disrupted Blockbuster).
- Generative AI consulting (threatening professional services 2025-2026).

### 4.3. Innovator's Dilemma

**Christensen 1997 book**:
- Successful incumbents fail responding disruption.
- Listening current customers = ignoring future market.
- Resource allocation favors high-margin = blocks disruption.

### 4.4. Modern relevance 2025-2026

**Examples 2025-2026**:
- **BYD + Chinese EVs** eating Western automakers share.
- **Generative AI** threatening consulting + professional services.
- **Open-source AI** disrupting closed-source.

### 4.5. Aplicacao em content marketing

**Disruptive Innovation content angles**:
- "**Christensen 2 types disruption** — low-end + new-market".
- "**NOT breakthrough technology** — accessible + affordable".
- "**BYD EVs + GenAI consulting** — modern disruption examples".
