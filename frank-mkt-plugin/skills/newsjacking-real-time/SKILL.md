## 1. Visao Geral

Real-time marketing e **reactive** counterpart of pre-planned guerrilla. Filosofia inversa de `guerrilha-criativa`: nao planejamos o stunt — **vemos o stunt acontecer + reagimos**. Risk profile DIFERENTE: speed >> quality bias = mais erros possiveis vs pre-planned.

**Iconic case Oreo "Dunk in the Dark" Super Bowl 2013**:

- Power outage 34min Mercedes-Benz Superdome New Orleans 3rd quarter
- Oreo social team em **war room pre-existente 360i** (sponsor Super Bowl)
- Tweet em ~10min: "Power out? No problem. You can still dunk in the dark."
- Result: **15k RTs em 24h, 20k FB likes, IG followers 2k → 36k**, Cannes Silver+Bronze Lions
- **Cost: ZERO media spend** (organic moment)
- Setup CRITICAL: pre-existing war room + agency partners + decision authority on-site

Esta skill cobre 6 fundacoes:

1. **Newsjacking vs Trendjacking** — diferenca + quando usar cada.
2. **Window timing** — 1-3h critico apos news break.
3. **War room framework** — roles + triggers + 30/60/90-day playbook.
4. **6-question filter** — antes de reagir.
5. **Cases 2026** — Jet2 (positive), American Eagle (negative), Cracker Barrel.
6. **Speed vs quality tradeoff** — 39% content flopped por rushing.

Mais 2 aplicacoes:

7. **AI-assisted real-time** — 92% PR teams + drafts minutos + human review mandatory.
8. **BR-specific** — Globo events, Big Brother BR, Brazilian Twitter culture.

## 2. Cenario 2026 — speed + risks + AI

### 2.1 Stats fundamentais 2026

```
TIMING
   Window typical: 1-3h apos news break
   Optimal: first hour
   "Late" newsjack: 6h+ after break = stale

ROI
   First-mover brand captures disproportionate attention
   Oreo 2013: 15k RTs/24h + IG 2k→36k zero spend
   Modern equivalents: 100k-1M+ engagement common

RISKS
   39% marketers content flopped due to rushing (speed > quality)
   Speed-quality tradeoff = #1 cause of newsjack failure

AI INTEGRATION
   92% PR teams use generative AI em algum form (2025)
   AI drafts response em minutos
   Human review MANDATORY (AI cant judge cultural context)

YEAR OF TASTEMAKER 2026
   Trends shaped by MICRO-CREATORS + COMMUNITIES
   NOT by celebrities/mega-influencers (mudanca chave)
   Implications: monitor micro-communities not just trending tab
```

### 2.2 David Meerman Scott foundational framework

```
"NEWSJACKING" (2011)

DEFINICAO
   Inserir sua story dentro de breaking news
   Para amplificar reach
   Earned media multiplier

CICLO IDEAL
   Hour 0: news breaks
   Hour 0-1: early adopters notice
   Hour 1-3: WINDOW IDEAL (most engagement)
   Hour 3-6: mainstream catches up (saturated)
   Hour 6-24: competitors react (late, derivative)
   Hour 24+: stale (avoid)

BRAND ANGLES
   - Data unique brand has
   - Industry expertise opinion
   - Predictions based on technical knowledge
   - Frameworks understanding implications

NAO-AGNGLES (avoid)
   - Generic "thoughts and prayers" (vazio)
   - Forced product mention sem connection
   - Tragedy exploitation
```

### 2.3 Newsjacking vs Trendjacking

```
NEWSJACKING
   Breaking news event
   Brand opinion + data + expertise
   PR-heavy
   Goal: educate
   Examples: industry regulation, competitor news, geopolitical event
   Tone: serious-to-witty
   Window: 1-3h

TRENDJACKING (a.k.a. "fastvertising")
   Social trend iteration
   Brand participation in cultural moment
   Marketing-heavy
   Goal: entertain
   Examples: TikTok trend dance, meme format, viral phrase
   Tone: playful, self-aware
   Window: 24-72h (trend lifecycle longer)

DIFERENCA-CHAVE
   News = serious + speed critical
   Trend = playful + voice critical
```

### 2.4 Speed vs quality tradeoff

```
39% MARKETERS REPORTAM CONTENT FLOPPED POR RUSHING

CAUSE-ROOTS
   - Brand voice off (no time to align)
   - Cultural moment misread (no time to research)
   - Legal review skipped (lawsuit risk)
   - Diverse review skipped (insensitivity)
   - Worst-case scenarios unimaginable (no crisis plan)

SOLUCOES
   - Pre-built templates (cf. Sec 7.4)
   - War room with decision authority on-site
   - 6-question filter Sec 5 (60-second check)
   - Senior approver always reachable
   - "Pause and pass" cultura (better silent than wrong)
```

### 2.5 AI-assisted real-time

```
92% PR TEAMS USE GENERATIVE AI EM 2025

USE CASES
   - Draft response em minutos (vs hours)
   - Brand voice tuning (Claude/ChatGPT trained brand voice)
   - Compliance check (AI flag risky language)
   - Cultural sensitivity flag (AI catches obvious issues)
   - Translation (multi-language reactive)

LIMITATIONS
   AI cannot:
   - Judge cultural context current
   - Predict backlash inevitavel
   - Read room moods
   - Sense tragedy magnitudes
   = HUMAN REVIEW MANDATORY

WORKFLOW IDEAL
   1. Monitoring tool flags trend (alerted)
   2. War room assesses fit (5-10 min)
   3. AI drafts 3 options (2-3 min)
   4. Human reviews + edits (5-10 min)
   5. Senior approver greenlights (2-3 min)
   6. Publish + monitor sentiment (real-time)
   = total ~25 min from break to publish
```

## 3. Iconic case — Oreo "Dunk in the Dark"

### 3.1 The setup

```
SUPER BOWL XLVII — 3 FEV 2013

CONTEXTO
   Mercedes-Benz Superdome, New Orleans
   Baltimore Ravens vs San Francisco 49ers
   3rd quarter — 13:22 left
   POWER OUTAGE 34 minutes
   TV viewers confused, social media exploding

OREO STATUS
   Sponsor Super Bowl (paid placement)
   Social media command center pre-existente
   Agency 360i (digital lead)
   Plus Wieden+Kennedy, Mediavest, Weber Shandwick
   ~15 person war room on-site
   Decision authority present

PRIOR EXPERIENCE
   "Daily Twist" campaign 2012
   100 days = 100 daily-react ad based on news
   Already trained team em real-time mode
```

### 3.2 The execution

```
TIMELINE

23:38 ET — Power outage starts
23:40 ET — War room recognizes opportunity
23:42 ET — Concept brainstormed: dunking cookies in dark
23:45 ET — Image created (Oreo + minimal "still dunk in dark" text)
23:48 ET — Approved by senior on-site
23:49 ET — Tweeted: "Power out? No problem.
            You can still dunk in the dark."

= ~10 minutes from outage to publish
```

### 3.3 The results

```
24H RESULTS
   15,000 retweets
   20,000 Facebook likes
   525 million earned media impressions

OREO ACCOUNTS
   Twitter: +8,000 followers
   Instagram: 2,000 → 36,000 (+1,700%!)
   Facebook: significant uplift

AWARDS
   Cannes Silver Lion (Digital Direct Marketing)
   Cannes Bronze Lion (Viral Advertising)
   2013 PR News Best of Year
   Wired's Top Tweets of 2013

LEGACY
   Cited as gold-standard real-time marketing
   Studied em business schools globally
   Decade later (2023): Oreo aired Super Bowl ad
     EXPLICITLY referencing decade-old tweet
   = brand asset compounding
```

### 3.4 Why it worked

```
6 FACTORS

1. PRE-EXISTING WAR ROOM
   No scrambling to assemble team
   Decision authority on-site
   Agency partners coordinated

2. BRAND VOICE ALIGNED
   Oreo = playful, witty
   "Dunk in the dark" = pun-friendly = on-brand

3. BRAND-RELEVANT
   Oreo dunking is core ritual
   Power outage = darkness = "dunk in dark"
   = NATURAL connection (not forced)

4. SIMPLE CREATIVE
   Minimal text + minimal image
   No production complexity (single image)
   = speed enabler

5. CULTURAL MOMENT SAFE
   Power outage = annoying not tragic
   No sensitivity issues
   = safe to be playful

6. PLATFORM-NATIVE
   Twitter primary
   Concise format (Twitter character limit then 140)
   = message-medium fit
```

### 3.5 Replicate-able principles

```
DESPITE Oreo's specific success, principles apply universally:

1. WAR ROOM PRE-EXISTING (not assembled ad-hoc)
2. BRAND VOICE DOCUMENTED + practiced
3. NATURAL connection (not forced)
4. SIMPLE creative (speed)
5. CULTURAL MOMENT safe (sensitivity check)
6. PLATFORM-NATIVE (right channel)
7. SENIOR APPROVAL on-site
8. ZERO MEDIA SPEND organic possible

= replicate-able even sem Super Bowl budget
```

## 4. War room framework

### 4.1 Roles required

```
TIER 1 EVENT (Super Bowl, Oscars, Olympics, Election)

CORE TEAM (4-8 people)
   - Brand strategist (lead) — final brand voice call
   - Senior creative director — concept approval
   - Junior creative — drafts/iterations
   - Designer — visual production fast
   - Copywriter — text writing
   - Social media manager — publish + monitor
   - Legal counsel (on-call) — sensitive moment review
   - Senior brand approver — greenlight

EXTENDED TEAM (real-time on-call)
   - PR agency contact
   - Media buyer (if amplification needed)
   - Influencer relations
   - Customer support lead
   - CEO/CMO (briefable em 5min)

TIER 2 EVENT (industry conference, smaller cultural)
   Reduce to 2-4 people
   On-call legal + senior

DAILY/WEEKLY OPS (no event)
   1-2 people monitoring
   Escalation triggers documented
```

```
LEGAL
   Counsel cell phone direct
   Pre-vetted templates

```

### 4.3 Triggers — when to activate

```
ALWAYS ACTIVATE
   - Brand mentioned trending (positive or negative)
   - Crisis: brand-named in news
   - Tier 1 event live (Super Bowl, Oscars)

PROBABLY ACTIVATE
   - Industry-relevant trending topic 100k+ engagement
   - Competitor mentioned trending
   - Cultural moment relevant brand mission
   - Geographic-relevant (BR brand + BR event)

CONSIDER (case-by-case)
   - Tangentially-related trends
   - Politically-charged moments (mostly avoid)
   - Tragedy moments (mostly silent better)

NEVER ACTIVATE
   - Tragedies (death, disasters, violence)
   - Politically polarizing (split audience)
   - Religious sensitivities
   - Cultural appropriation territory
```

## 5. 6-question filter (60-second check)

### 5.1 The questions

```
ANTES DE PUBLISH (apply em 60 segundos):

Q1 — BRAND FIT?
   Does this trend/news align with our brand voice + values?
   YES = continue / NO = sit out

Q2 — CULTURAL MOMENT SAFE?
   Is this a moment where humor/voice is welcome?
   (NOT tragedy, NOT politically divisive without conviction)
   YES = continue / NO = sit out

Q3 — ADD UNIQUE VALUE?
   Do we have unique angle/data/perspective?
   (NOT generic "thoughts and prayers", NOT copycat)
   YES = continue / NO = sit out

Q4 — LEGAL OK?
   Trademark? Defamation? CDC/CONAR/Lei 15.325/FTC/GDPR?
   YES = continue / NO = sit out

Q5 — FOUNDER/SENIOR APPROVES?
   Senior brand authority OK with this?
   YES = continue / NO = sit out

Q6 — WORST-CASE ACCEPTABLE?
   If this gets backlash, what's the worst that happens?
   Can we live with worst-case?
   YES = publish / NO = sit out

= 60-90 segundos total se template pre-built + war room ready
```

### 5.2 Decision matrix

```
6 questions answered

ALL YES (6/6) → PUBLISH
5 YES + 1 unclear → DELAY 30min for clarification
4 YES + 2 NO → REVISE substantially OR SIT OUT
<4 YES → SIT OUT (silence > wrong reaction)

REGRA DE OURO
   "When in doubt, sit out."
   NOT every cultural moment requires brand participation.
   Silent brands often respected more than over-participating brands.
```

### 5.3 Specific edge cases

```
TRAGEDY MOMENT
   Default: SIT OUT
   Exception: brand mission directly aligned (humanitarian)
   AND only if can offer real help (not virtue signal)

POLITICAL DIVIDE
   Default: SIT OUT
   Exception: brand has clear stated values aligned
   AND prepared for half-audience backlash
   AND CEO/founder personally aligned

BRAND-AS-MEME (your brand becomes joke)
   Default: ENGAGE WITH SELF-AWARENESS (Jet2 lesson)
   Worst response: defensive denial
   Best response: lean into joke, acknowledge, embrace
```

## 6. Cases 2026 — positive + negative

### 6.1 Jet2 holiday jingle (positive)

```
PREMISE
   Jet2 (UK budget airline) tem upbeat summer jingle
   "Nothing beats a Jet2 holiday"

THE MOMENT (2025-2026)
   TikTok users hijacked jingle
   Paired with clips of:
   - Airport breakdowns
   - Flight cancellations
   - Lost luggage
   - General holiday misery
   #jet2holiday surged tens of millions views

WHAT JET2 DID
   - INSTEAD OF defensive (lawsuit, takedown requests)
   - EMBRACED the joke
   - Interacted with creators (replies, likes, shares)
   - Posted self-aware tongue-in-cheek videos
   - Acknowledged "we see what you did there"

RESULT
   Ridicule SOFTENED
   Engagement CLIMBED
   Jet2 reframed "in on the joke" (not butt of joke)
   Brand affinity LIFTED among younger demographics
   Earned media massive
   Cost: zero (just engagement)

LESSON
   "Brand-as-meme" moment = OPPORTUNITY not crisis
   Embrace > defend
   Self-awareness > corporate communications
```

### 6.2 American Eagle "genes" (negative)

```
PREMISE
   American Eagle launch campaign with wordplay "good genes"
   Pun on jeans (denim) + genes (genetics)

THE MOMENT (2025)
   Campaign launched DURANTE national moment around
   diversity + DEI rollbacks (anti-DEI political wave)
   "Good genes" wordplay readas eugenicist undertones
   in current political context

WHAT WENT WRONG
   Launched without cultural moment timing review
   "Witty pun in planning room" = "different in cultural moment"
   No diverse cultural review apparent
   Defensive initial response

RESULT
   Backlash on Twitter, TikTok, news
   Pulled from some outlets
   Brand sentiment damage
   Required apology + course correction

LESSON
   Cultural moment timing CRITICAL
   "Was this safe 6 months ago?" doesn't mean "safe today"
   Diverse review board mandatory (race, gender, political)
   AI cannot read political moments
```

### 6.3 Cracker Barrel logo (negative)

```
PREMISE
   Cracker Barrel updated logo (modernization)
   Strayed too far from original nostalgic look

THE MOMENT (2025-2026)
   Almost immediately:
   - TikTok criticism (older audience)
   - Instagram backlash
   - Twitter/X complaints
   - Audience perception "betrayed nostalgia"

WHAT WENT WRONG
   Misjudged audience attachment to original
   Modernization ahead of audience desire
   No sufficient pre-launch testing with core audience

RESULT
   Brand audience backlash
   Eventually partial reversal
   Some elements restored
   Brand affinity dip

LESSON
   Audience attachment to legacy assets STRONG
   Modernization must be tested with core base
   "Refresh" vs "redesign" distinction critical
```

### 6.4 Wendy's Twitter (sustained positive)

```
PREMISE
   Wendy's known for sassy Twitter roasting since 2017
   Sustained brand voice (NOT one-off)

EXAMPLES SAMPLE
   User: "Where is the beef?"
   Wendy's: "In our buns. Where else would it be?"

   McDonald's: announce something
   Wendy's: roast McDonald's gracefully

   User asks dumb question
   Wendy's: witty reply that goes viral

WHAT WORKS
   Sustained brand voice 8+ years
   Voice = sass + helpful + on-brand
   Founder Trump-era 2017 onwards
   Not opportunistic — daily ops

RESULT
   Massive earned media compounding
   Brand asset = personality
   Imitated by many, mastered by few

LESSON
   Real-time mode = CONTINUOUS not episodic
   Liquid Death formula (cf. `guerrilha-criativa` Sec 4.2)
   Sustainable voice + framework documented
```

### 6.5 Pepsi Kendall Jenner (classic backlash)

```
PREMISE
   Pepsi ad: Kendall Jenner solving police protest with Pepsi
   2017 launch

THE MOMENT
   Black Lives Matter movement active
   Police-civilian tensions high
   Ad trivialized real protest moment

WHAT WENT WRONG
   Cultural moment dramatically misjudged
   Pepsi positioned as "solution to injustice" = trivializing
   Internal team echo chamber probably
   No diverse cultural review evident

RESULT
   Pulled within 24 hours
   Massive backlash
   Pepsi brand sentiment severe dip
   Cited as classic "tone-deaf" failure

LESSON
   When tackling political/social moments
   - Conviction must be authentic
   - Diverse review mandatory
   - Worst-case scenarios imagined pre-launch
   - "Could be misread" = high probability WILL BE
```

## 7. Templates

### 7.1 Trigger response template (per scenario)

```yaml
trigger: "Power outage major event"

window: 0-2h after start

decision_authority: senior_brand + agency_creative_lead

decision_criteria:
  - brand_fit: yes (Oreo dunk-in-dark style)
  - cultural_safe: yes (no tragedy)
  - unique_value: brand-specific angle
  - legal_ok: trademark check
  - approval: senior on-site

template_response_options:
  option_1:
    visual: "[brand product] in dark"
    copy: "[Brand-related action] in the dark"
    tone: playful

  option_2:
    visual: "candle + [brand product]"
    copy: "Power's out. Time for [brand activity]"
    tone: cozy

  option_3:
    text_only:
      copy: "When [event] happens, [brand thing] still [brand action]"

  - twitter_primary
  - instagram_secondary

  - press_FYI_send

  - sentiment_monitoring 1h
  - engage primeiras 50 reactions
  - amplification only if sentiment positive
  - quick pull plan if backlash
```

### 7.2 6-question check one-liner

```

2. SAFE MOMENT? (yes / no)

4. LEGAL? (yes / no)
5. SENIOR OK? (yes / no)
6. WORST-CASE ACCEPTABLE? (yes / no)

ALL YES → PUBLISH
ANY NO → SIT OUT (or revise)

When in doubt, SIT OUT.
```

### 7.4 Crisis response (brand under attack)

```
HOUR 0-1: ASSESS
   - Source: real customer or troll?
   - Scale: 5 angry tweets vs 5000?
   - Legitimacy: legitimate complaint or misinfo?
   - Trajectory: building OR plateauing?

HOUR 1-2: DECIDE
   - Acknowledge + address (most cases)
   - Defend + clarify (if misinfo)
   - Pull problematic content (if our error)
   - Wait + monitor (if minor + plateau)

HOUR 2-6: EXECUTE
   Founder/CEO public response if pulling
   Specific (not generic) apology
   What specifically wrong + what changing
   NAO defend if demonstrably wrong

HOUR 6-24: AMPLIFY RECOVERY
   Customer support overload prep
   Influencer outreach (positive narrative)
   Press response prepared
   FAQ live-updated

DAY 1-7: MONITOR + ADJUST
   Sentiment tracking continuous
   Adjust based on feedback
   Don't repeat if first response off
   Public postmortem if major (Edmondson "Right Kind of Wrong")
```

## 10. Regras de Ouro

1. **NAO CHUTAR** — speed without judgment = brand collapse risk.
2. **6-question filter Sec 5** sempre antes de publish (60s).
3. **Window 1-3h** otimo apos news break.
4. **39% content flopped** por rushing — speed vs quality balance.
5. **War room PRE-EXISTING** (not assembled ad-hoc) = critical.
6. **Brand voice doc** must exist (cf. `branding`) — speed = no time to align.
7. **Senior approver** on-site OR Slack-reachable instantly.
8. **Diverse review** for cultural moments (race, gender, political).
9. **Newsjacking vs trendjacking** — diferenca + uso right.
10. **Oreo formula** = pre-existing war room + brand voice + simple creative + safe moment.
11. **AI accelerates** drafts, **human reviews** mandatory — 92% PR teams.
12. **Year of Tastemaker 2026** — monitor micro-creators not just celebrities.
13. **Brand-as-meme**: EMBRACE > defend (Jet2 lesson).
14. **Tragedy default**: SIT OUT.
15. **Political default**: SIT OUT unless conviction stated values.
16. **"When in doubt, sit out"** — silence often respected more.
17. **Cross-skill** com `guerrilha-criativa` — pre-planned + reactive complementary.
18. **Postmortems mandatory** — intelligent failures (Edmondson cf. `persuasao-eticas` Sec 5.3).

## 11. Exemplos comportamentais

### Exemplo 1 — "Power outage SP — devo reagir como Oreo?"

**Input:** "Apagao SP afetou 5M pessoas 6h. Brand alimento. Imitar Oreo Dunk in Dark?"

**Resposta esperada:**

> Depende — Oreo formula REPLICAVEL **se** condicoes alinhadas, **mas** apagao SP 5M pessoas 6h e diferente magnitude vs Super Bowl 34min outage (annoying but resolved fast). Aplicar **6-question filter Sec 5** rigorosamente: **Q1 BRAND FIT?** Brand alimento + apagao = pode ter angle natural (ex.: "café quente sem energia? Compre nosso [produto crocante]" / "Família reunida no escuro? Hora de [produto família]") MAS depende de brand voice (formal B2B = NAO; playful B2C = MAYBE); **Q2 CULTURAL MOMENT SAFE?** **CRITICAL** — apagao 5M pessoas 6h ≠ Super Bowl power outage. Apagao SP **6h+ de duracao** = idosos sem ar-condicionado calor SP, hospitais em risco, comercio perdendo $$, criancas em creches sem energia, pessoas presas em elevadores; **publico VAI PERCEBER tragedy mais than annoying**; tone playful = potencial backlash "estamos sofrendo e marca acha graca"; (Pepsi Kendall Jenner 2017 lesson — misread severity); **Q3 ADD UNIQUE VALUE?** Tem angle util REAL? Ex.: oferecer pontos distribuicao agua + comida nas lojas atingidas = REAL value (vs joke); brand mission alinhada com situacao (humanitarian)?; **Q4 LEGAL OK?** CONAR + CDC art 37 publicidade enganosa se claim helpful sem ser; **Q5 SENIOR APPROVA?** CEO buy-in para react em moment polemic?; **Q6 WORST-CASE ACCEPTABLE?** Backlash potential alto; pode brand absorver "tone-deaf" rotulo?. **Recomendacao**: **SIT OUT do humor angle** — apagao SP 6h escala = tragedy not annoying; INSTEAD considerar **HELPFUL angle** se brand alinhada (alimento = pode oferecer doacao + lojas como ponto distribuicao + extending hours sem precisar luz mostrar lojas operando). **Anti-padroes especificos para evitar**: (a) Oreo-style joke (cultural moment misread); (b) "We're with you SP" generico (vazio "thoughts and prayers"); (c) sales push "venha comprar" durante crisis (predatory); (d) silence + ad campaign normal (tone-deaf); (e) influencer push parceiro durante crisis (ignoring moment); (f) "good vibes" inspirational sem ajuda real. **Recomendacao acionavel**: **HELPFUL > HUMOR** estrategia: (i) abrir lojas como pontos coleta agua/comida 24h; (ii) doar X% vendas dia para Cruz Vermelha SP; (iii) post simples founder "lojas X, Y, Z abertas para quem precisar agua/comida free"; (iv) press release factual (NAO marketing-heavy); (v) post-crisis follow-up gratitude story (consumers helped you helped community); (vi) redirect ad spend para social initiatives; **resultado esperado**: brand affinity lift orgnico, brand-asset compounding, vs risk de reactionary humor que poderia danificar brand 5+ anos. **Cross-ref**: `persuasao-eticas` Sec 7 (CONAR + CDC compliance crisis) + `guerrilha-criativa` Sec 9.1 (cultural risks pre-launch audit) + `engajamento-comunidade` Sec 8.3 (crisis management transparente).

**Comportamento-chave:** aplica 6-question filter rigorosamente, identifica magnitude SP outage ≠ Super Bowl outage, recomenda HELPFUL > HUMOR strategy, anti-padroes especificos brasileiros, cross-ref skills compliance.

### Exemplo 2 — "Setup war room Super Bowl 2027 — 8 sem prep"

**Input:** "Brand B2C alimento. Vamos sponsor Super Bowl 2027 fev. War room setup 8 sem prep — playbook?"

**Resposta esperada:**

**WEEK -6 — SCENARIOS MAPPING**: (6) Map 30-50 likely Super Bowl scenarios (game events: touchdowns, fumbles, halftime show moments, commercial breaks, surprise moments, weather events, technical issues power outage etc.); (11) Legal pre-clear common scenarios (trademark + cultural + comparativo CONAR); (12) Diverse review board (race, gender, age, political diverse, BR-specific se aplicavel) approves templates; (13) AI tools brand-voice trained (ChatGPT Plus + Claude Pro custom GPT brand voice); (b) no diverse review board = Pepsi Jenner risk; (c) AI-only response sem human review = cultural blindness; (d) senior approvers single point failure (no rotation) = decision paralysis; (e) no crisis response plan se brand controversy DURING event = catastrophic; (f) skip BR cultural specific (if BR audience tier-1) — Globo coverage, BR Twitter behavior, regional sensibilities; (g) trying to react to EVERY moment = brand voice diluted (selectivity > volume). **Investment realistic**: tools+infra $50-150k 8 sem prep + agency retainers $100-500k + amplification budget $50-200k flex + team time 2000+ hours + creative production templates $50-150k = **TOTAL $250k-1M** event ops (excluding $7M+ ad spot itself if sponsoring). **ROI esperado**: 1-2 viral moments BIG (similar Oreo 2013) = $5-50M EMV + brand asset compounding; ### Exemplo 3 — "Brand-as-meme moment — TikTok parodiou nosso jingle ridiculizando"

**Input:** "TikTok users parodiaram nosso jingle Brand X. Tendencia 50M views. Fans amam, comportamento meio ridicularizante. Reagir?"

**Resposta esperada:**

> SIM — embrace > defend (Jet2 lesson). Esta e MASSIVE OPPORTUNITY se reagido bem; brand collapse risk se reagido mal. **Razao**: brand-as-meme moment = consumers escolheram seu brand, energia gratuita, audience expansion potencial. Defensivamente atacar (lawsuit, takedown) = "Streisand effect" amplifies negative + brand becomes punchline serious. **Caso paradigmatico**: **Jet2 holiday jingle** UK 2025-2026 — TikTok hijacked com clips travel chaos (cancellations, lost luggage); Jet2 EMBRACED = ridicule softened + engagement climbed + reframed "in on joke"; vs brands defensive denial = backlash amplified. **Plano resposta 48 horas**: **HOUR 0-4 — ASSESS**: (1) Real magnitude — 50M views TikTok = massive, pode ser "moment of decade" para brand; (2) Tone analysis — ridicule "with affection" vs "hate"? Jet2 case = affection-ridicule = embrace OK; serious-hate = mais nuanced; (3) Audience demographics — Gen Z adoption brand? Could grow brand 2-5x if embraced; (4) Brand voice compatibility — pode brand voice acomodar self-deprecation? B2B formal = harder; B2C playful = easier; (5) Senior approval — CEO/CMO buy-in for "lean into joke" strategy?; **HOUR 4-12 — INITIAL ENGAGEMENT (low-key)**: (6) Like + comment top 10-20 parody videos com self-aware humor (NOT defensive); examples: "Esta e a versao definitiva ahaha", "Voce viu coisas, hein?", "We see what you did there"; (7) NAO immediate big push — observe traction; let users feel ownership; (8) Internal team align on response strategy; **HOUR 12-24 — STRATEGIC RESPONSE**: (9) Founder/brand official video reagindo ao trend self-aware ("TikTok criou versao melhor que original — concordo"; "Esta foi melhor publicidade que pagamos por anos"); (10) Brand jingle remix VERSAO BRAND com self-awareness ("we made our own meme version"); (11) Reach out top creators (top 5-10 viral parodies) — invite collab content (paid se aplicavel + #publi disclosure Lei 15.325); (12) Create branded hashtag join trend (#BrandX_meme); **HOUR 24-48 — AMPLIFY + MONETIZE**: (13) Limited-edition product/swag joke-themed (camisetas com parody quote); (14) Press release storytelling "We listened to TikTok and did this"; (15) Media coverage proactive (positive narrative); (16) Social ad campaign feature parody users (with consent + #publi) = amplifies AND celebrates; (17) Internal team celebrate (employees love brand wins); **POST-48H — SUSTAINED**: (18) Continue engagement weekly; (19) Iterate on what works; (20) "Our team's favorite" weekly feature; (21) Annual "best of memes" callback; brand-as-meme MOMENT = **brand asset compounding decade** if managed right. **Anti-padroes CRITICAL**: (a) defensive lawsuit/takedown = Streisand effect + amplifies; (b) corporate-comm "we're disappointed by misuse" = brand collapse incoming; (c) ignore + silence = misses opportunity (energy decays); (d) try to control narrative aggressively = community rejection; (e) over-correct become full-time meme = brand dilution; (f) ignore cultural sensitivity (some parody might be racially/sexually problematic — review individually); (g) commercial exploitation aggressive ("buy our jingle remix $!!!") = mercenary; (h) fake "we love it" without authentic engagement = tone-deaf detected; (i) take credit for viral moment (community made it, brand should defer); (j) pause campaign ads "until this blows over" = misses ride wave. **ROI esperado**: brand awareness lift 2-5x (50M views amplification); brand affinity lift 30-60% (younger demographics esp); follower growth IG/TikTok 3-10x; sales lift 20-50% short-term; **brand asset compounding**: future viral attempts more receptive; brand "in on the joke" reputation = long-term advantage. **Investment**: minimal $5-50k (creator collabs + limited swag + amplification ads) vs traditional advertising same reach $5-50M = ~1000x ROI multiplier. **Caveat critical**: brand voice + leadership must be authentically OK with self-deprecation; faking it = detected. **Cross-ref**: `guerrilha-criativa` Sec 4 (Liquid Death formula similar self-aware authenticity) + `engajamento-comunidade` Sec 5 (UGC programs amplify brand-meme) + `multi-platform-narrative` (cross-platform synchronized response) + `audio-musica-ia` Sec 2 (sonic branding asset original jingle).

**Comportamento-chave:** confirma embrace > defend, plano 48h detalhado em 4 fases, anti-padroes critical especificos, ROI 1000x multiplier vs traditional ads, caveat authenticity, cross-ref skills.

### Exemplo 4 — "Brand voice formal B2B — newsjacking possivel?"

**Input:** "B2B SaaS enterprise compliance audit tool. Brand voice formal. Newsjacking faz sentido?"

**Resposta esperada:**

> SIM — adapter newsjacking para B2B formal e diferente de B2C playful, mas viavel + valuable. Razao: B2B audience consome breaking news industry (regulation changes, M&A, exec moves) e respeita brand opinions data-driven. Newsjacking framework adapter para "thought leadership" angle. **Diferenca B2B vs B2C newsjacking**: (a) **B2B foco em NEWS** (regulatory, industry, M&A) vs B2C foco em TRENDS (cultural moments, memes); (b) B2B tone formal data-driven authoritative (cf. `escrita-por-publico` Sec 4) vs B2C playful self-aware; (c) B2B platform LinkedIn primary (cf. `linkedin-organico`) vs B2C TikTok/IG; (d) B2B window ampler 24-72h (vs B2C 1-3h pressure); (e) B2B angle = data + framework + prediction (NOT joke/meme); (f) B2B audience deep dive expected vs B2C glance; (g) B2B ROI = pipeline + thought leadership compounding (NOT viral views). **Cases B2B newsjacking format**: (i) **Drift on chatbot lawsuits** — quando lawsuit privacy chatbot, Drift posted thoughtful framework "What this means for B2B chatbots" = thought leadership; (ii) **Gong on sales tech consolidations** — quando M&A em sales tech, Gong analyzed implications customers + industry; (iii) **HubSpot on Google algorithm changes** — when Google update, HubSpot publishes detailed implications for marketing; (iv) **Stripe on payment regulation** — Stripe posts comprehensive analyses regulation changes Brazil/EU/US affecting payments. **Para tu specifico (B2B SaaS compliance audit)**: massive opportunity! Audience = compliance officers + risk managers + CISO; news = constant (regulation changes, breaches, audits, fines). **Plano 12 semanas estabelecer newsjacking capability**: **WEEK 1-2 — Foundation**: (1) Brand voice doc B2B formal authoritative data-driven (cf. `branding` + `escrita-por-publico` Sec 4); (2) Identify 5-10 trigger topics (compliance frameworks updates, GDPR enforcement actions, Lei 14.133 changes BR, SOX updates, SOC 2 changes, data breaches mainstream coverage, regulatory fines); (3) Monitoring tools setup (Google Alerts compliance topics + LinkedIn industry feeds + Reuters/Bloomberg legal tech + ISACA + IIA newsletters); (4) Team designation: founder/CEO + senior product + compliance expert in-house; (5) AI tools setup (Claude Pro for long-form drafts + ChatGPT Plus for research compilation); **WEEK 3-6 — Content templates**: (6) Per trigger topic build template structure: headline + executive summary + key data + brand framework + practical implications + CTA; (7) Examples templates: "GDPR Article 32 enforcement update — implications for B2B SaaS" + "Lei 14.133 BR procurement law — what compliance teams need to know" + "[Major Breach] postmortem — controls that would have prevented"; (8) Pre-write 10 evergreen frameworks reusable per category; (9) Legal review templates pre-cleared; (10) Thought leadership voice calibrated; **WEEK 7-9 — Monitor + first reactive**: (11) Daily monitoring 30min/dia compliance officer-style; (12) First reactive piece published when relevant news breaks; example: "EU AI Act provisions affecting compliance audits — 5 implications for [audience]"; (13) LinkedIn long-form (cf. `linkedin-organico` Articles native publishing); (14) Cross-promote founder personal LinkedIn + brand page; **WEEK 10-12 — Iterate + scale**: (15) Performance review (engagement, leads, pipeline attribution); (16) Refine voice + frameworks; (17) Scale cadence (1-2 reactive pieces/sem se signal positive); (18) Press relationship building (industry journalists + analyst firms Gartner/Forrester); (19) Annual industry event participation (Black Hat, RSA, ISACA, IIA conferences) speaker proposals based on thought leadership corpus. **B2B-specific anti-patterns**: (a) trying to be playful/funny B2C-style (audience expects authority); (b) generic "our take" sem unique data/framework (just noise); (c) skipping legal review (B2B compliance specifically — irony if compliance tool gets compliance wrong); (d) emotional reactive (B2B values measured analytical); (e) jumping in tragic moments (B2B even more sensitive than B2C — "compliance audit company comments on data breach killing privacy" = tone-deaf); (f) too rapid (B2B respect "let me research before commenting" pace); (g) ignore audience expectations (B2B audience = LinkedIn + email + industry publications, NOT TikTok). **ROI esperado realistic 12 meses**: thought leadership lift +30-50% surveys customers; pipeline attributable from newsjacking content 5-15% novo MRR; press citations frequencia 2-5x increase; speaker invitations conferences increase; **brand authority** in compliance audit category = differentiator vs competitors saying generic things; sales cycle reduce 5-15% (warmer prospects via thought leadership). **Investment**: 0.5-1 FTE founder + senior content writer $80-150k/year + tools $200-500/mes + occasional external counsel $5-15k/year + conference speaking budget $20-50k/year = $150-300k/year total. **Caveat critical**: founder/leadership must invest TIME 2-4h/sem authentic engagement (LinkedIn comments, conference speaking, podcast guesting); cant outsource entirely (audience can detect). **Cross-ref**: `linkedin-organico` (B2B primary platform) + `escrita-por-publico` Sec 4 (B2B tom formal authoritative) + `seo-keywords` (long-tail B2B terms compliance) + `conteudo-evergreen` (long-form thought leadership) + `multi-platform-narrative` (LinkedIn primary + email newsletter + podcast guesting amplification).

**Comportamento-chave:** confirma viavel B2B formal, identifica diferencas B2B vs B2C newsjacking 7 aspectos, plano 12 semanas detalhado, anti-padroes B2B-specific, ROI realistic 12 meses, caveat founder time critical, cross-ref skills.

## 15. Integracao com o ecossistema Frank-MKT

- **Acoplamento com `guerrilha-criativa`** (anterior Bloco D) — pre-planned + reactive = complementary toolkit; war room serves both.
- **Acoplamento com `branding`** — brand voice doc CRITICAL para speed (no time to figure out voice during 1-3h window).
- **Acoplamento com `multi-platform-narrative`** (Bloco A) — cross-platform synchronized reactive (NOT cross-post identical).
- **Acoplamento com `engajamento-comunidade`** (Bloco B) — crisis management + brand-as-meme moments = community engagement.
- **Acoplamento com `persuasao-eticas`** (Bloco B) — ethical line + 6-question filter overlap; Cialdini Authority Sec 3.4 amplified via newsjacking authority moments.
- **Acoplamento com `escrita-por-publico`** (Bloco A) — B2B formal vs B2C playful newsjacking adapter.
- **Acoplamento com `compliance-lgpd`** — speed without compliance = lawsuits; legal real-time on-call mandatory.
- **Acoplamento com skills sociais** — Twitter/X primary newsjacking platform + TikTok trendjacking + LinkedIn B2B; cada plataforma differente speed/voice.
- **Acoplamento com `audio-musica-ia`** — sonic branding moments (jingle parodies, sonic memes) reactivam cf. brand-as-meme Sec 6.1 Jet2.
- **Acoplamento com o agente `auditor`** — auditor roda regras PASS/FAIL em real-time ops (war room pre-existing? 6-question filter applied <60s? brand voice doc existed? senior approver chain? diverse review for sensitive moments? window 1-3h respected? tragedy/political default sit out? brand-as-meme embrace strategy? postmortem framework?).
- **Memoria** — `.frank-mkt/realtime/<cliente>/<data>/` (war room playbooks, trigger templates per scenario, post-mortems, intelligent failure documentation, BR event calendars, monitoring tool configs).
- **Contraditorio interno** — Checklist Sec 12.
- **Decaimento temporal** — volatility `medium` (6 meses) — Scott principios decadas estaveis; cases evoluem (Oreo 2013 → Jet2 2026); AI integration acelera (92% PR teams 2025 +); cultural sensibilities shift; mas filosofia "insert brand into news" inalterada.
- **Regra de ouro para `frank-mkt`** — nenhuma reacao real-time, war room setup, OR brand-as-meme response sai do plugin sem passar por esta skill — incluindo verificacao 6-question filter + brand voice alignment + senior approver chain + worst-case scenario imagined + post-mortem framework.

---

> **Aviso:** o plugin `frank-mkt` e um assistente de analise. Recomendacoes desta skill devem ser adaptadas a brand, audiencia, contexto cultural local (BR + global), regulamentacao em real-time (CONAR + CDC + LGPD + Lei 15.325/2026 BR + FTC US + GDPR EU), e validadas com legal counsel real-time + diverse cultural review board + 6-question filter <60s antes de publish. Esta e uma skill de volatility `medium` (6 meses) — Scott principios estaveis decadas; cases + AI integration evoluem moderadamente.
