## 1. Fundacao 1 — ICP vs Buyer Persona — Distinção crítica

### 1.1. Definitions atualizadas 2026

**ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**:
- Defines **organizational characteristics** (company-level).
- Firmographics + buying triggers + market trends.
- "Where to find them and when to reach out".
- B2B context primary.

**Buyer Persona**:
- Describes **individuals** (person-level).
- Role + motivations + challenges + communication preferences.
- "Who to talk to and how to engage".
- B2B + B2C context.

**Critical distinction 2026**:
- **ICP**: which accounts to target.
- **Persona**: how to engage people inside accounts.
- ICP=where + when; Persona=who + how.

### 1.2. ICP framework 3-part 2026 (Sybill)

**Component 1 — Firmographics** (static data):
- Revenue.
- Headcount.
- Geography.
- Industry vertical.
- Technology stack.
- Example: "B2B SaaS $10M-$50M ARR running Salesforce".

**Component 2 — Triggers** (proxy for urgency):
- Hiring new VP Sales.
- Series B raised.
- Missed quarterly earnings.
- "**Where there is chaos, there is budget**".

**Component 3 — Macro trends**:
- Industry tailwinds.
- Regulatory changes.
- Technology shifts.
- AI adoption pressure.

### 1.3. Business impact ICP defined 2026

**Stats macro**:
- B2B SaaS com ICP defined: **68% higher win rate** (SiriusDecisions).
- **36% higher retention**.
- **38% stronger win rates**.
- Marketing contributes significantly more revenue.

**Why critical 2026**:
- Answer engines (ChatGPT+Perplexity+Gemini) discovery.
- Cannot clearly define = **invisible at moment purchase intent**.

## 2. Fundacao 2 — JTBD Framework 2026

### 2.1. JTBD origins + frameworks

**Tony Ulwick**:
- Founder Strategyn.
- **Original creator JTBD 2002**.
- ODI (Outcome-Driven Innovation) methodology.

**Clayton Christensen**:
- Harvard Business School.
- **Popularized JTBD** post-2003.
- Theory + conceptual.

**Bob Moesta**:
- Demand-Side Sales.
- Practical methodology.

**Distinção**:
- Christensen = **theory** (conceptual).
- Ulwick + Moesta = **methodology** (steps + actions).

### 2.2. Ulwick ODI — 86% success rate

**Outcome-Driven Innovation**:
- **86% success rate** across hundreds engagements.
- vs **industry average 17%**.
- 5x improvement vs traditional methods.

**Tools built into ODI**:
- **Opportunity Algorithm** (quantitative).
- **Outcome-based segmentation**.
- **JTBD Growth Strategy Matrix**.

### 2.3. JTBD principle

**Core thesis**:
- People buy products **to get a job done**.
- Job = task + goal + objective.
- "**Hire**" product = solution.
- "**Fire**" product = bad solution.

**Distill demand**:
- Identify **unmet customer needs**.
- Find **most underserved**.
- "Where people willing pay more get job better".

### 2.4. Real-world examples 2026

**Airbnb**:
- Job: NOT just sleeping accommodation.
- Job: **authentic local experiences + sense of belonging**.
- Disrupted traditional hospitality.

**Fitbit**:
- Job: NOT tracking fitness data.
- Job: **manage + improve personal health**.
- Insights + motivation healthier living.

**Zoom**:
- Job: **connect remotely peers + coworkers**.
- "Hired" achieve goal.
- Pandemic accelerated.

### 2.5. JTBD interview technique

**5 forces (Bob Moesta)**:
- **Push** (current situation pain).
- **Pull** (new solution attraction).
- **Anxiety** (fear new solution).
- **Habit** (current solution familiarity).
- **Outcome** (desired result).

- Investigating solutions.

- Evaluating specific vendors.

**Stage 4 — Decision**:
- Selecting solution.
- Negotiating terms.

**Stage 5 — Onboarding**:
- Implementing solution.

## 4. Fundacao 4 — Customer Interviews 2026

### 4.1. Interview types

**Exploratory** (discovery):
- Map current landscape.
- Workflows + pain points + behaviors.
- "What is the situation?"

**Generative** (innovation):
- Motivations + aspirations + unmet desires.
- Uncovering opportunities.
- "What could be?"

**Usability** (validation):
- Test specific designs.
- "Does this work?"

**Feedback** (optimization):
- Refine existing solutions.
- "How can we improve?"

### 4.2. Best practices 2026

**Preparation + planning**:
- Define **clear research objectives**.
- Recruit **right participants** (ICP fit).
- Craft **thoughtful open-ended questions**.

**Interview technique**:
- **Curiosity > telling**.
- "Listen more than you talk".
- **Resist proposing solutions**.
- "What if we built X" = shifted discovery → validation.
- Stay neutral + let participants speak.

### 4.3. Continuous discovery principle

**Modern shift 2026**:
- Single round usability = snapshot in time.
- True momentum = **sustainable ongoing dialogue**.
- Robust feedback loop informs roadmap.
- Teresa Torres "continuous discovery habits".

### 4.4. AI synthesis platforms 2026

**Modern tools**:
- **Quals AI** (real human interviews + insights 24h).
- **Conveo AI** (qualitative research software).
- **Listen Labs** (AI qualitative).
- **Dovetail** (research repository + AI tags).

**AI does**:
- Transcription + translation.
- Pattern detection.
- Theme identification.
- Quote extraction.

**Human does**:
- Design research.
- Verify themes.
- Synthesize insights.
- Make decisions.
