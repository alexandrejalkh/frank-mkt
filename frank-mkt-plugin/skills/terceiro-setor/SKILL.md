## Fundacao 1 — Nonprofit Communication Framework 2026

**Mission + Theory of Change** = foundation. Antes copy, antes campaign, antes Glassdoor, antes anything: defina o que muda no mundo se sua ONG existe + como.

**Theory of Change Logic Model** (Center for Theory of Change + W.K. Kellogg Foundation):
1. **Long-term outcome** — mudanca sistemica final (ex: erradicar trabalho infantil regiao X)
2. **Intermediate outcomes** — mudancas medias (ex: 1000 criancas escola integral)
3. **Outputs** — produtos imediatos (ex: 10 escolas equipadas + 100 educadores treinados)
4. **Activities** — acoes (ex: treinamento + doacao material + advocacy publica)
5. **Inputs** — recursos (ex: R$ 2M/ano + 12 funcionarios + 200 voluntarios)
6. **Assumptions** — hipoteses logicas (ex: educadores treinados = qualidade ensino)
7. **External factors** — fora controle (ex: politica publica municipal estavel)

**Stakeholder map terceiro setor** (Kotler + Drucker non-profit):
1. **Doadores** — individuais (small + major + recurring) + corporate (CSR + cause marketing) + foundations (grants) + government (Lei MROSC + Lei Rouanet + Lei Incentivo Esporte)
2. **Beneficiarios** — populacao servida (DIGNIDADE primeiro: nao prop fotografico)
3. **Funcionarios** — equipe paga (salarios mercado, NAO low-pay-because-cause)
4. **Voluntarios** — engajamento + retencao + skills-based volunteering
5. **Conselho/Board** — governanca + fundraising + strategic direction
6. **Comunidade** — local + tematica (ex: comunidade saude mental)
7. **Governo** — federal + estadual + municipal (politicas publicas + parcerias MROSC)
8. **Midia** — earned coverage + crisis exposure
9. **Parceiros peers** — outras ONGs (cooperation > competition)

**Communication framework 4-stage** (CCS Fundraising 2026 + Candid):
1. **Awareness** — descoberta da causa + problema tangible
2. **Education** — entendimento profundidade + theory of change
3. **Engagement** — primeira acao (sign petition + small donation + volunteer)
4. **Sustained relationship** — recurring giving + advocacy + legacy

**Personalization + segmentation 2026** (CCS + Funraise):
- Major donors (R$ 50k+/ano) — high-touch concierge + impact reports trimestrais + founder access
- Recurring donors (mensal) — automated stewardship + impact updates + community
- One-time donors — re-engagement journey + monthly giving upgrade campaign
- Lapsed donors — win-back sequence + survey "porque saiu"
- Volunteers — skills-based opportunities + recognition
- Corporate partners — quarterly business reviews + co-branded campaigns
- Foundation/grant officers — program reports + outcome data + audit-ready

**50-30-20 revenue diversification rule** (Funraise 2026):
- 50% recurring (monthly + corporate partnerships + recurring grants)
- 30% project-based (specific campaigns + matching gifts + crowdfunding)
- 20% strategic relationships (major donors + legacy + endowment)

**Brasil specificity comm framework**:
- Linguagem: nao mendiga, nao caridade, NAO "ajude carente"
- Codigo: dignidade beneficiario + protagonismo populacao servida
- LGPD: doadores PII protegida + consent base legal explicita
- Lei 12.965/2014 Marco Civil + Lei 13.709/2018 LGPD = compliance obrigatoria

## Fundacao 2 — Impact Reporting (AA1000 + SROI + IRIS+ + Theory of Change)

**AccountAbility AA1000 Principles 2018** (accountability.org):
1. **Inclusivity** — engagement stakeholders relevantes em decisoes
2. **Materiality** — identificacao questoes mais significativas
3. **Responsiveness** — agir e comunicar sobre questoes raised
4. **Impact** — monitorar outcomes acoes tomadas

**AA1000 Standards Series**:
- **AA1000APS (AccountAbility Principles Standard)** — framework foundational
- **AA1000AS (Assurance Standard)** — Type 1 (principles assurance) vs Type 2 (principles + performance assurance)
- **AA1000SES (Stakeholder Engagement Standard)** — engagement processes

**SROI (Social Return on Investment)** — quantifica valor social criado vs investimento (GrantStation + SROI Network):
- **SROI ratio** = present value of impact / value of inputs
- Exemplo: SROI 5:1 = cada R$ 1 investido gera R$ 5 valor social
- 7 principles SROI: stakeholders + outcomes + materiality + monetary value + transparency + verify + don't over-claim
- **Limites SROI**: sensivel a assumptions monetary value (deadweight + attribution + drop-off + displacement)
- Casos Brasil: Fundacao Lemann + Itau Social usam variantes SROI

**IRIS+ metrics (GIIN — Global Impact Investing Network)**:
- 600+ standardized metrics impact investing + nonprofit
- Categorias: poverty + education + health + climate + gender + financial inclusion
- Free public catalog (iris.thegiin.org)
- Compatibility: SDG-aligned + ESG-compatible

**Theory of Change vs Logframe**:
- **Theory of Change** — narrative + assumptions + external factors (mais qualitativo)
- **Logframe (Logical Framework)** — matriz objetivo + outcomes + outputs + activities + indicators (mais quantitativo)
- Use ambos: ToC para storytelling + Logframe para reporting

**Impact reporting framework 4 layers** (Sopact 2026):
1. **Inputs** — what you invested (R$ + horas + volunteers)
2. **Outputs** — what you produced (X services + Y trainings + Z events)
3. **Outcomes** — what changed for stakeholders (Z% literacy improved + W% income increase)
4. **Attribution** — evidence of causal change (control group + counterfactual + RCT)

**Common pitfalls impact reporting** (JFW Accounting + Sopact):
- Output-only (X services delivered) sem outcome (Z% lives changed)
- Vanity metrics (1M followers) sem behavior change
- Cherry-pick best stories, omit failures
- No baseline (cant prove change)
- No control group (correlation != causation)
- Aggregate hide variation (medias mascaram desigualdade)

**SORP 2026 Brasil + UK reference** (SJC Chester):
- UK SORP (Statement of Recommended Practice) 2026 trustees annual reports new compliance
- Brasil ainda nao tem SORP equivalente formal MAS GIFE Codigo de Etica + ABONG Carta de Principios = padrao informal
- Auditoria externa OSCIP/OS obrigatoria (Lei 9.790/1999 + Lei 9.637/1998)
- LGPD donor data + beneficiary data compliance

**Mensuracao Tier 1 nonprofit**:
- **Donor retention rate** (target 60%+ year 1, 80%+ year 5+)
- **Donor lifetime value (LTV)**
- **Cost per dollar raised (CPDR)** — target <0.20 (R$ 0.20 cost per R$ 1 raised)
- **Program efficiency ratio** — program expenses / total expenses (target 75%+)
- **SROI ratio** — annual
- **Outcome metrics specific** (lives changed, behavior shifts, system changes)

## Fundacao 3 — Corporate Philanthropy Strategic (Porter & Kramer + CSR vs CSV)

**Porter & Kramer Strategic Philanthropy 2002** (HBR seminal):
- "The Competitive Advantage of Corporate Philanthropy" (Dec 2002 HBR)
- Argumento: filantropia melhor quando alinhada com **competitive context** empresa
- 4 elementos competitive context: factor conditions + demand conditions + related industries + strategy/rivalry
- Exemplo: Cisco Networking Academy = filantropia (educacao IT) + ROI (talent pipeline + market expansion)
- NOT charity ad-hoc, NOT random causes, NOT "porque CEO gosta"

**Porter & Kramer CSV (Creating Shared Value) 2011** (HBR seminal):
- "Creating Shared Value" (Jan 2011 HBR) — evolucao Strategic Philanthropy
- CSV vs CSR: CSR = compliance/reputation/charity, CSV = business model integration
- 3 metodos CSV: reconceiving products+markets + redefining productivity value chain + enabling local cluster development
- Exemplo Nestle: rural sourcing programs aumentam quality + farmer income + brand purpose
- Exemplo Walmart: supply chain sustainability = cost reduction + emission reduction
- Critica academica: CSV pode ser CSR rebrandeado se nao fundamental

**CSR vs Corporate Philanthropy distinction** (360MatchPro):
- **CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility)** — broader umbrella, includes ethics + environment + governance + philanthropy
- **Corporate Philanthropy** — specific subset = monetary + in-kind donations + employee giving + cause marketing
- ESG = framework moderno (Environment + Social + Governance) — stakeholder reporting

**Strategic philanthropy 2026 trends** (Double the Donation + Givinga):
- **Skills-based volunteering ROI** — talent retention + community impact + employee engagement
- **Workplace giving integrated** — payroll deduction + matching gifts + dollars-for-doers
- **Larger grants fewer partners** — concentration > spray-and-pray
- **Measurable outcomes mandatory** — boards demand impact data not just $ raised
- **AI-supported impact measurement** — IRIS+ data integration + SROI calculation tools
- **Climate + DEI + community empowerment** top causes 2026

**CSR steady-or-growing 2026** (NonProfit PRO):
- Most companies expect CSR budgets STEADY or GROW
- Refining HOW + WHERE invest externally (more strategic)
- Concentration: fewer nonprofit partners + bigger grants
- Pressure: prove ROI + alignment business priorities

**Anti-pattern corporate philanthropy**:
- Random causes ("CEO daughter likes turtles")
- Greenwashing (sponsorship sem business model change)
- Wash-rinse-repeat ad-hoc giving
- No measurement (just $ donated, no outcome tracking)
- Performative ally (Pride month rainbow logo, no internal LGBTQ+ policies)
- Crisis-only PR (after scandal donate to apologize)

**Brasil corporate philanthropy ecosystem**:
- **GIFE 130+ associados** — Grupo Institutos Fundacoes Empresas (gife.org.br)
- **R$ 4+ bi/ano** investimento social privado GIFE associados
- **Lei Rouanet** (Lei 8.313/1991) — incentivo cultura

## Fundacao 4 — Cause Marketing 2026 (Patagonia + TOMS + Dove + Brasil cases)

**Cause marketing definition**: alianca for-profit + nonprofit que beneficia ambos (vendas + brand purpose + causa funding).

**Patagonia model "Earth is our only shareholder" 2022**:
- 2022 founder Yvon Chouinard transferiu 100% ownership $3B company para trust + foundation

- Brand purpose = business model fundamental (CSV Porter+Kramer textbook case)

- 2006 Blake Mycoskie launched "buy one give one" model (shoes)

- **Critique academica**: dependency creation + local market disruption + paternalism + sostenibility questionable
- 2019 TOMS pivoted: 1/3 profits give to grassroots groups (pivoted away from one-for-one shoe)

**Dove Real Beauty 2004-presente**:
- 2004 launched "Real Beauty" campaign (Ogilvy)
- Self-esteem girls + women + mothers

## Fundacao 7 — Anti-Greenwashing/Woke-Washing/Virtue Signaling

**Definicoes** (Marketing Society + Edelman + KPMG):
- **Greenwashing** — claim ambiental sem sustancia (ex: BP "Beyond Petroleum" while still oil major)
- **Woke-washing** — claim social/DEI sem sustancia (ex: Pride logo sem politicas LGBTQ+)
- **Virtue signaling** — performative posturing (ex: black square Instagram BLM 2020 sem hiring black leaders)
- **Performative ally** — ally aparente sem acao consequente
- **Purpose-washing** — brand purpose superficial sem business model integration
- **Pinkwashing** — gay-friendly aparente para outras agendas (ex: Israel Tel Aviv Pride critica)
- **Rainbow-washing** — Pride month logo rainbow sem rest of year support

**Como detectar** (Edelman Trust Barometer + KPMG ESG Reporting):
1. **Vague language** ("committed to sustainability") sem metrics
2. **Year-of-launch only**, sem track record 3+ anos
3. **No third-party audit** ou questionable methodology
4. **Internal contradictions** (oil company "renewable" while expanding drilling)
5. **Selective reporting** (highlight wins, hide losses)
6. **Disconnection internal-external** (Pride campaign sem trans health insurance)
7. **Crisis-only philanthropy** (donate after scandal)

**Backlash 2025-2026** (Marketing Society):
- Bud Light Dylan Mulvaney 2023 = $1B+ market cap loss + sustained boycott
- Disney "woke" backlash + Florida political fight
- Target Pride 2023 backlash + sales decline
- Patagonia/Ben & Jerry's = standout because **integrated** purpose (not surface)

**Anti-greenwashing framework** (KPMG + Sustainability Directory):
1. **Concrete measurable goals** (ex: "reduce 50% emissions 2030 vs 2020 baseline")
2. **Third-party audit** (Big 4 + AA1000AS + ISAE 3000 standards)
3. **Public data + dashboard** (audit trail visivel)
